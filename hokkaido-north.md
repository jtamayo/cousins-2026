# Trip C: True North
### Hokkaido — 12 days

![Hokkaido summer landscape — wide open skies and rolling green terrain](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_560,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1512444240/hokkaido/Hokkaido1363_2)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/hokkaido/hokkaido/)*

The weather guarantee. This is the only itinerary in this entire set that genuinely sidesteps Japan's rainy season — Hokkaido is the one major region that doesn't get tsuyu. In early-to-mid June, Sapporo is cool and clear, the alpine meadows are turning green, the volcanic peaks still have snow on their upper slopes, and the island has roughly 15 hours of daylight. Add in Japan's best food city, one of the most dramatic volcanic landscapes on earth, a fox and deer population you'll encounter almost daily, and some of the country's most serious hiking, and you have a trip that competes with Iceland or the Scottish Highlands without the uncertainty that comes with every beach-focused June itinerary.

This is not the Japan most people visit. It's better.

---

## At a glance

- **12 days / 11 nights**
- Tokyo (3N) → Sapporo + Otaru day trip (2N) → Sounkyo / Daisetsuzan (2N) → Biei + Furano (2N) → Lake Toya + Niseko (2N) → fly home from New Chitose
- 1 domestic flight, 1 car-based circuit
- **Best timing: Early-to-mid June** (June 1-20). This is the ideal window — cooler temperatures, zero rainy season, fewer tourists, alpine flowers, long daylight.
- Late June also works but is slightly more crowded and warmer.
- This is the best of the five options if you can travel in the first two weeks of June.

---

## What makes this trip worth skipping Kyoto

Most experienced Japan travelers have done Kyoto, Osaka, the shinkansen bullet train, ryokan with communal onsen. Hokkaido is genuinely different. The landscape is European — rolling hills, dairy farms, canola fields, volcanic cones. The food is Japanese but Hokkaido-specific: miso ramen invented here, soup curry invented here, the dairy is incomparable, the seafood (sea urchin, crab, scallops) is extraordinary. The outdoor scale is larger. The sense of being on a frontier island — Hokkaido was only settled in the 19th century; the Ainu indigenous culture predates that by centuries — is different from the ancient-civilization feeling of Honshu. And the foxes on the roadsides are a recurring highlight that never gets old.

## Section by section

### Tokyo (3 nights)

Three nights of jet lag and personal agendas — the group has been here before, so this is not a highlights tour. Kappabashi kitchen street for those with a knife and ceramic obsession. Shimokitazawa and Koenji for vintage clothing and independent records. Ueno Zoo for the animal-inclined. One strong group dinner, booked in advance.

### Sapporo + Otaru (2 nights)

Japan's most underrated food city, by the judgment of many Japanese food obsessives. Miso ramen was invented here — eat it at a counter in Ramen Yokocho late at night. Soup curry is a Sapporo original: a deeply aromatic, complex broth with whole chicken leg and a forest of roasted vegetables, unlike anything else in Japan. The sea urchin rice bowl at Nijo Market for breakfast. Genghis Khan grilled lamb at Daruma in Susukino — loud, smoky, joyful. The Sapporo Beer Museum in its old brick factory.

Then, a 40-minute train along the Ishikari Bay coast to Otaru: Japan Sea sushi at a counter on Sushi Street (amberjack, snow crab, sea urchin caught that morning, roughly a third of Tokyo prices), and the LeTAO double-fromage cheesecake — a two-layer affair of baked and raw cream cheese that is remarkably good. Buy several boxes.

### Sounkyo Gorge / Daisetsuzan (2 nights)

![Hokkaido's dramatic volcanic and alpine interior](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_500,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1515929604/hokkaido/Hokkaido1504_4)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/hokkaido/hokkaido/)*

Drive 2.5 hours northeast from Sapporo into Daisetsuzan National Park — Japan's largest, a vast volcanic wilderness at the center of Hokkaido. Sounkyo is a canyon cut by the Ishikari River into volcanic rock, with walls rising 100–200 meters above the road and ryokan built into the cliff face. In early June, the twin waterfalls — Ryusei and Ginga — are at full strength. The kaiseki dinners here pull from Hokkaido's interior pantry: river fish, mountain vegetables, local venison.

The next morning is the serious mountain day. The Asahidake Ropeway carries you to 1,600 meters in 10 minutes, where the trail emerges onto a volcanic plateau of steam vents, sulfurous pools, and dwarf pines. From there, the summit of Asahidake — Hokkaido's highest peak at 2,291m — is 5–6 hours round trip. In early June there may be snow patches on the upper slopes, and the summit views on a clear day sweep from the Japan Sea to the Pacific. For those who prefer the ropeway without the full climb, the plateau alone is extraordinary — alien landscape, wild ptarmigan common, panoramic views in every direction — and you're back at the ryokan in time for lunch.

### Biei + Furano (2 nights)

![Lavender fields and rolling farmland, Furano, Hokkaido](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_500,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1508552277/hokkaido/Hokkaido1497_3)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/hokkaido/hokkaido/)*

The Biei highlands are the landscape that made Hokkaido famous in Japan: rolling hills patchworked with lavender, wheat, canola, and wildflowers, cut through with farm roads and solitary iconic trees — a lone elm in the middle of a field, an oak on a hillside — that have become famous in Japanese photography. In early June the hills are intensely green with yellow and blue accents, and red foxes appear on field edges with regularity. Rent bikes in Biei town and ride the Patchwork Road circuit — 15km through fields and farmland at a pace that suits the landscape.

The Blue Pond at Shirogane is not exaggerated: aluminum hydroxide from natural mineral springs mixes with river water to create a blue that looks digitally altered and is not. Visit at 6am before anyone else arrives. The submerged dead birch trees visible through the perfectly transparent water make the composition almost unreasonably good. Furano's Farm Tomita and the valley road round out the day. Stay in a farmhouse guesthouse and eat together in the kitchen — the night sky in rural Biei is extraordinary.

### Lake Toya + Niseko (2 nights)

![Lake Toya — volcanic caldera lake with Mt. Usu reflected on its surface](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_500,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1516979956/hokkaido/Hokkaido1449_10)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/hokkaido/hokkaido/)*

Lake Toya is a volcanic caldera lake with an island at its center and an active volcano on its southern shore. The town runs nightly fireworks over the lake from late April through October — 10 minutes, every evening, not a special event, simply something they do. The Mt. Usu Ropeway shows the volcanic crater and views to the Pacific. Showa Shinzan — a mountain that emerged from flat farmland during eruptions in 1943–45 — is a 20-minute drive and as strange as it sounds.

Then Niseko, 45 minutes west: quiet, green, the volcanic cone of Mt. Yotei enormous on the horizon. Luxury hotels here run at summer prices — a fraction of what they cost in ski season. One night at the Park Hyatt Niseko or Ritz-Carlton Niseko Village is the trip's splurge, and it delivers: private bath facilities, exceptional food, the whole group together in one property. White-water rafting on the Shiribetsu River for those who want it. Fresh soft-serve ice cream and cheese at the Hokkaido dairy farms for everyone.

## Getting there and back

**SFO → Tokyo:** Nonstop on ANA, JAL, or United (~11 hrs) into Haneda.

**Tokyo → Sapporo:** Domestic flight, Haneda to New Chitose (CTS), ~1h45m. The busiest domestic air route in Japan. ANA and JAL visitor fares (Experience Japan / Japan Explorer Pass) run 5,500-11,000 yen per segment.

**Hokkaido circuit:** Rent a car (or large van for 6) at New Chitose Airport on Day 4 or Sapporo on Day 6. You drive the circuit: Sapporo → Sounkyo → Biei/Furano → Lake Toya/Niseko → back to New Chitose. The whole loop is roughly 600km over the Hokkaido days.

**Departure:** New Chitose → Haneda (~1h45m), then connect to SFO nonstop.

## Day-by-day

### Day 1: Arrive Tokyo
Land, check in, easy nearby dinner.

### Day 2: Tokyo — focused
Morning: Juan leads Kappabashi kitchen street. Steph splits to Shimokitazawa.
Afternoon: Eliza to Omotesando or Ueno Zoo; Phil + Christian: running + explore.
Evening: Strong group dinner, booked ahead.

### Day 3: Tokyo — free
Full free day. Whatever the group needs.

### Day 4: Fly to Sapporo
Morning: Last Tokyo morning.
Afternoon: Fly Haneda → New Chitose (~1h45m). Pick up rental car(s) at the airport OR take the shuttle to Sapporo and pick up cars later.
Check in. Evening: Nijo Market is open late; explore Susukino.

### Day 5: Sapporo + Otaru
Morning: Train to Otaru. Sushi Street lunch (this meal should be on a weekday — counters are less crowded). Canal walk. LeTAO cheesecake. Return to Sapporo by mid-afternoon.
Evening: Choose one: soup curry dinner (Garaku), miso ramen in Yokocho, or Genghis Khan at Daruma. You can't do all three in one night but try.

### Day 6: Drive to Sounkyo
Pick up cars (if not already). Drive 2.5 hours to Sounkyo Gorge through the Hokkaido interior.
Arrive by early afternoon. Walk the gorge path under the canyon walls. Ryusei and Ginga waterfalls are 5km from the town (flat walk or short drive).
Check in at the ryokan. Private kashikiri bath booked for Juan & Steph. Full kaiseki dinner.

### Day 7: Asahidake
*Summit group* (Phil, Christian, Juan, Steph, anyone up for it): Drive to the ropeway base (~40 min from Sounkyo). Ropeway at opening (~8am). Begin hiking from the 1,600m station. Summit and return: 5-6 hours.
*Plateau group* (Eliza, Lillian, anyone who prefers): Ropeway up, volcanic plateau walk, steam vents, ptarmigan spotting, ropeway down, explore the gorge town, onsen afternoon.
Reunite for dinner.

### Day 8: Drive to Biei
Morning departure. 2-hour drive south to Biei.
Check in. Afternoon: Patchwork Road circuit by bike (rent in Biei town) or by car with frequent stops.
Evening: Biei town has a handful of good restaurants. Or cook at the guesthouse/Airbnb.

### Day 9: Biei + Furano
Very early morning: Shirogane Blue Pond before tourists arrive. The light before 7am is extraordinary.
Morning: Drive the Panorama Road and Farm Road circuits, rolling fields and tree photography.
Afternoon: Drive to Furano. Farm Tomita. Furano cheese factory.
Return to Biei for the night.

### Day 10: Drive to Lake Toya
Morning drive (~2.5 hours) to Lake Toya.
Check in. Afternoon: Mt. Usu Ropeway + Showa Shinzan.
Evening: Waterfront ryokan dinner. Nightly fireworks over the lake (~8-9pm, ~10 minutes).

### Day 11: Drive to Niseko / Splurge night
Morning: Lake Toya at leisure. Boat to Nakajima island if interested.
Midday: Drive to Niseko (~45 min - 1 hr). Check into the splurge hotel.
Afternoon: White-water rafting on Shiribetsu (Phil + whoever), Niseko onsen hopping, cycling, Hokkaido dairy farm visit.
Evening: Best dinner of the Niseko stay.

### Day 12: Drive to New Chitose, fly home
Morning: Drive to New Chitose (~2 hours). Return rental car.
Fly CTS → Haneda (~1h45m). Connect to SFO nonstop.
Allow 3+ hours connection time at Haneda for the international transfer.

## What to book before you leave

1. **Car rental** — Large van (Toyota HiAce class, 8-seater) or 2 compact cars, picked up at New Chitose on Day 4. Small fleet, book early.
2. **International Driving Permits** for drivers — from AAA before departure.
3. **Sounkyo ryokan** with private kashikiri bath access — confirm tattoo policy and private bath availability when booking
4. **Splurge hotel in Niseko** (Park Hyatt, Ritz-Carlton Niseko Village, or Skye Niseko) — summer rates are much lower than winter but still book ahead for 3 rooms
5. **Asahidake Ropeway** — no advance booking required, just show up. But check June operating hours closer to the trip.
6. **Biei Airbnb / guesthouse** — farmhouse accommodation that takes the whole group, ideally
7. **Otaru sushi counter** — some of the best counters take reservations; walk-in at lunch usually works but call ahead for 6 people

## Key risks

**No warm water.** This is the clearest trade-off. If Christian or Phil arrive wanting to swim in tropical ocean, this trip doesn't deliver. The Hokkaido coast in June is cold. The trade-off is reliable weather, dramatic outdoor experiences, and exceptional food. Be explicit with the group about this before choosing.

**Driving circuit logistics.** Six people, ~600km of Hokkaido roads over 6 days. The roads are excellent, well-signed, and driving in Hokkaido is genuinely pleasant. The expressway network is modern. But you need at least 2 drivers with International Driving Permits, and you need to rent 2 cars or one large van. Budget for expressway tolls and fuel.

**Lillian / pregnancy and onsen.** Hot spring onsen are contraindicated in pregnancy due to hyperthermia risk. She can't use communal baths or even private kashikiri if water is hot. The group should factor this in — she'll sit out or do a different activity during onsen time. This is not a trip-killer; the onsen portions are evenings at the ryokan and she can enjoy the meals, the atmosphere, and the rest of the program.

**Tattoos.** Communal onsen at ryokan ban tattoos. Private kashikiri baths are the solution — book them explicitly when you reserve your Sounkyo accommodation and the Niseko splurge. Most properties offer them; a few are tattoo-tolerant even in communal baths (more common in Hokkaido than Honshu). Ask directly.

**Early June vs. late June distinction.** The optimal window for this trip is June 1-20. After June 20, Hokkaido starts getting warmer, slightly busier, and some properties shift to peak summer pricing. Asahidake's snow patches disappear, which is neither better nor worse. The flowers in Biei are at their greenest mid-June. If you can't travel until late June, this trip still works well — but the early-June window is the unique advantage.
