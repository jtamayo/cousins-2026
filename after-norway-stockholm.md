## Why Stockholm

- Direct flights from Bergen (~1.5h, SAS/Norwegian) and from Alesund (via Oslo, ~3–4h)
- **Midsommar falls on June 19–20 in 2026** — Sweden's biggest holiday, and you'd arrive right on time (see below)
- Bigger and more varied than Copenhagen — built across 14 islands, with more museums, more neighborhoods, and the archipelago
- Excellent vegetarian food scene
- Great running routes along the waterfront and through Djurgarden
- Beautiful in late June: 18+ hours of daylight, warm enough to be outdoors constantly
- Good onward connections for J&S (direct flights to Istanbul ~3.5h, and most of Europe)

## Midsommar (June 19–20)

Swedish Midsummer is the country's most celebrated holiday — arguably bigger than Christmas. Midsummer Eve is **Friday, June 19** in 2026, with celebrations continuing into Saturday the 20th.

**What happens**: Swedes raise and dance around a maypole (majstång), wear flower crowns, eat herring and strawberries, drink snaps, and sing traditional songs. The whole country basically shuts down and moves outdoors. It's joyful, photogenic, and deeply communal — exactly the kind of "something happening in a city" that Christian likes.

**Where to experience it in/near Stockholm**:
- **Skansen** (open-air museum on Djurgarden) — the most accessible Midsommar celebration in Stockholm. Traditional maypole raising, folk music, dancing. Very popular, arrive early
- **Stockholm Archipelago** — take a ferry to one of the islands (Möja, Sandhamn, Vaxholm) for a more local, intimate celebration. This is how Stockholmers actually do it
- **Hagaparken** or other city parks often have smaller celebrations

**Planning note**: Because Midsommar is a major holiday, many restaurants and shops close or have reduced hours on Jun 19–20. Plan accordingly — stock up on food/drink, or book restaurant reservations well ahead. The state liquor store (Systembolaget) sees its biggest sales day of the year right before Midsommar.

## What to do

### Museums & culture
- **Vasa Museum** — a fully intact 17th-century warship that sank on its maiden voyage and was salvaged 333 years later. One of the most visited museums in Scandinavia. On Djurgarden island
- **Fotografiska** — world-class photography museum in a waterfront Art Nouveau building. Good restaurant on the top floor
- **ABBA The Museum** — more fun than you'd expect, interactive, on Djurgarden near the Vasa
- **Moderna Museet** — Sweden's museum of modern art, on Skeppsholmen island
- **Nobel Prize Museum** — in Gamla Stan, small but interesting

### Neighborhoods
- **Gamla Stan** — the medieval old town on its own island. Narrow cobblestone streets, the Royal Palace, Stortorget square. Touristy but genuinely beautiful
- **Södermalm (Söder)** — the hip south island. Best thrift/vintage shopping, independent coffee shops, good bars. SoFo (south of Folkungagatan) is the core area
- **Östermalm** — upscale, food hall (Östermalms Saluhall), elegant streets
- **Norrmalm** — city center, department stores, Kungsträdgården park
- **Djurgården** — the green island. Vasa Museum, Skansen, ABBA museum, Rosendals Trädgård (garden cafe with organic food — great for vegetarians)

### Outdoors
- **Stockholm Archipelago** — 30,000 islands stretching into the Baltic. Day ferries to Vaxholm (~1h), Grinda (~2h), or Sandhamn (~2.5h). Swimming, hiking, kayaking, eating at island restaurants
- **Djurgården loop** — walk or run the waterfront around the island (~5km). Beautiful views of the city
- **Långholmen** — former prison island, now a park with swimming beaches and a hostel in the old cells
- **Hagaparken** — English-style park north of the center, with the Copper Tents and butterfly house
- **Metro art tour** — Stockholm's metro stations are famous for art installations (painted bedrock ceilings, mosaics, sculptures). Stations to see: T-Centralen (blue line), Solna Centrum, Kungsträdgården, Stadion

### Food & drink
- **Östermalms Saluhall** — upscale food hall, good for browsing and lunch
- **Rosendals Trädgård** — garden cafe on Djurgården, organic and vegetarian-friendly, eat in the greenhouse
- Strong coffee culture: Drop Coffee, Johan & Nyström, Kafé Esaias
- Vegetarian/vegan restaurants: Hermans (buffet with harbor views), Lao Wai, Mahalo
- **Fika** — the Swedish coffee-and-pastry ritual. Cinnamon buns (kanelbullar) and cardamom buns (kardemummabullar) everywhere

### Shopping
- **Södermalm** — best for thrift and vintage: Beyond Retro, Judits, Stockholms Stadsmission
- **Östermalm / Norrmalm** — higher-end shopping, Swedish design (Acne Studios, COS, & Other Stories are all Swedish)
- **Designtorget** — Swedish design gifts and homeware

## Practical notes

- **Getting there from Norway**: Bergen → Stockholm is ~1.5h direct (SAS). Alesund → Stockholm requires a connection via Oslo (~3–4h)
- **Getting around**: Walk + metro. The metro is excellent and covers everywhere. Ferries to the archipelago and Djurgården
- **Cost**: Similar to Copenhagen/Norway. Hotels ~$200–300/night, meals ~$20–40/person
- **Currency**: Swedish Krona (SEK), but cards accepted everywhere (Sweden is nearly cashless)
- **J&S onward**: Stockholm has direct flights to Istanbul (~3.5h), Copenhagen (~1.5h), Amsterdam (~2.5h), and most of Europe

## Stockholm vs. Copenhagen — quick comparison

| | Stockholm | Copenhagen |
|---|---|---|
| **Big event** | Midsommar (Jun 19–20) — Sweden's biggest holiday, maypole, flower crowns, archipelago parties | Sankt Hans Aften (Jun 23) — bonfires on beaches at sunset |
| **Event timing** | Day 1 after split — instant immersion | Day 4–5 after split — need to fill days before |
| **City size/variety** | Bigger, more spread out, 14 islands, archipelago | Compact, walkable, bikeable |
| **Museums** | Stronger (Vasa, Fotografiska, ABBA, Skansen) | Louisiana is world-class but a day trip |
| **Thrift shopping** | Very good (Södermalm) | Very good (Nørrebro/Vesterbro) |
| **Vegetarian food** | Excellent | Excellent |
| **Vibe** | Elegant, nature-woven, quieter | Cozy, bike-centric, livelier nightlife |
| **Day trip options** | Archipelago islands, Uppsala | Malmö (Sweden), Roskilde |
