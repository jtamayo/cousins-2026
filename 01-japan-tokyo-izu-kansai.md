# Option 1: Tokyo + Izu Peninsula + Osaka

The easiest group trip. Three nights in Tokyo to land and explore, five nights on the Izu coast for beaches, onsen, and seafood, then two nights in Osaka for a food-and-shopping finish. Nearly all surface transit — no domestic flights required.

## At a glance

- 11 days / 10 nights
- Tokyo (3N) → Shimoda / South Izu (5N) → Osaka (2N)
- 2 travel days with meaningful transit, no flights inside Japan
- Best for: group consensus, food lovers, couples who want easy split days

## The honest case for this option

**Why pick it:** This is the lowest-friction trip. Tokyo and Osaka are the two best cities in Japan for eating, shopping, and wandering. Shimoda gives you a genuine beach-and-coast stretch without flying anywhere. Every day has natural split points — couples who want to shop can shop, couples who want to hike a coastal trail can do that, and everyone regroups for dinner. If your group has never been to Japan together and you want the safest bet, this is it.

**Why not:** June is rainy season (tsuyu) across all of Honshu, and Shimoda's beach appeal takes the biggest hit. Beach facilities (lifeguards, beach houses, rental gear) don't fully open until mid-to-late July. Water temperature in June is 20-22C (68-72F) — swimmable with a wetsuit but not tropical. If several rainy days stack up during your Shimoda stretch, five nights there could feel long. This trip also skews city-heavy, which may underwhelm the more adventure-oriented members of the group.

## Getting there and back

**SFO → Tokyo:** Nonstop flights on United, ANA, JAL, and Zipair. About 11 hours. JAL or ANA to Haneda is ideal — Haneda is closer to central Tokyo.

**Osaka → SFO:** United operates a nonstop KIX-SFO route, but frequency is lower than the Tokyo corridor (typically once daily). Book early for six seats. Fallback: fly KIX → NRT domestically, then nonstop to SFO, which gives you many more options.

## How you move between sections

### Tokyo → Shimoda (Day 4)

Take the **Saphir Odoriko**, a premium limited express that runs direct from Tokyo Station to Izukyu-Shimoda in about **2 hours 40 minutes** with no transfers. It has large panoramic windows and optional private compartments — a genuinely enjoyable ride for a group. Regular Odoriko limited express trains also run direct at a similar time for less money.

The alternative is Tokaido Shinkansen to Atami (~50 min), then Izu Kyuko Line to Shimoda (~90 min with a transfer at Ito). Either way, this is a **half-day move**, not a full travel day. Depart Tokyo mid-morning, arrive by early afternoon, still have the evening free.

Cost: roughly 6,500-8,000 yen per person on the Saphir Odoriko. Note that JR Pass does not cover the private Izukyu Line portion (Atami to Shimoda, ~1,800 yen extra).

### Shimoda → Osaka (Day 9)

This is the longest surface move: roughly **4 to 4.5 hours** total.

Best route: take the Izukyu Line + JR Ito Line back to Atami (~1.5 hrs), then Tokaido Shinkansen (Hikari) from Atami to Shin-Osaka (~2.5 hrs).

Faster option: hire a private transfer from Shimoda to Mishima Station (~1.5 hrs by car, split among six people the cost is reasonable at ~12,000-15,000 yen total), then Shinkansen from Mishima to Shin-Osaka (~2 hrs). This cuts the total to about 3.5 hours.

Either way, treat this as a dedicated transfer day. Depart mid-morning, arrive in Osaka by late afternoon.

## Section by section

### Tokyo (3 nights)

Your landing zone. Use these days to adjust to the time zone, eat extremely well, and let each couple find their own rhythm before the group heads to the coast together.

**Neighborhoods to explore:**
- **Daikanyama / Nakameguro** — boutiques, cafes, tree-lined canal. Best for a morning walk with good coffee.
- **Shimokitazawa** — vintage shops, thrift stores, small bars. The best Tokyo neighborhood for shopping that isn't department stores.
- **Kiyosumi-Shirakawa** — third-wave coffee capital of Tokyo, plus a beautiful traditional garden.

**If you want an animal/nature branch:**
- Ueno Zoological Gardens — Japan's oldest zoo, in a great park with museums nearby
- Tokyo Sea Life Park — low-key aquarium with a good tuna tank, right on Tokyo Bay

**Dinner:** Plan one strong shared dinner night. Tokyo rewards reservation-based dining — pick a neighborhood (Ebisu, Shibuya, Shinjuku Golden Gai) and commit.

### Shimoda / South Izu (5 nights)

The trip highlight. A small, attractive port town on the southern tip of the Izu Peninsula, known for white-sand beaches, hot springs, and excellent seafood — especially kinmedai (golden-eye snapper).

**What makes Shimoda work:** It has the best beaches within reach of Tokyo by train. Shirahama Beach has genuinely white sand and clear water. Tatadohama is wider, more rugged, and popular with surfers. Perry Road is a short historic street with cafes and shops (Commodore Perry landed here in 1854). The pace is slow and the town is walkable.

**What to do:**
- **Beach days** at Shirahama or Tatadohama. Beaches are physically open in June but beach-house facilities (rentals, food stalls) don't fully set up until mid-July. Bring your own gear or rent from surf shops in town.
- **Surf lesson** — Tatadohama gets consistent Pacific swells. Wetsuit needed in June. Local surf shops rent boards and suits.
- **Coastal walking** — scenic cape and cliff routes along the south coast. Cape Irozaki (the southern tip of the peninsula) has dramatic cliffs and a botanical garden.
- **Day trip to Dogashima** (west coast, ~45 min drive) — stunning sea caves with boat tours through rock formations. This is a must if you have a car for a day.
- **Kawazu Seven Waterfalls** (Nanadaru, ~30 min north) — a lovely waterfall hike through a forested gorge.
- **Onsen** — the area is loaded with hot springs. Kanaya Ryokan has a famous riverside open-air bath.
- **Perry Road wandering** — cafes, small galleries, a good spot for a slower afternoon.
- **Snorkeling note:** Hirizo Beach, famous for clear snorkeling water, does not open its boat shuttle until July. In June, organized snorkeling is limited. Independent snorkeling is possible but water is cool and visibility varies with rain.

**Important correction:** The Shimoda Aquarium (Shimoda Kaichu Suizokukan) permanently closed in 2023. Do not plan around it.

**Is five nights too long?** For most travelers, yes — two to three nights is more typical for Shimoda. But for a group of six that wants a slow beach-and-onsen rhythm with cooking, reading, and unscheduled time, five nights is defensible. If the group leans more active, consider trimming to three or four nights and adding a night in Hakone (onsen + Fuji views) or an extra night in Osaka.

**Where to stay:** For three couples, a whole rental house is likely the best option. Shimoda has vacation rentals with ocean views, and June (pre-peak) availability should be good. Alternatively, for a splurge ryokan experience: **Arcana Izu** (high-end modern riverside retreat near Kawazu, ~30 min north) or **LOQUAT Nishiizu** (design-forward boutique on the west coast). Budget ryokans run 15,000-40,000 yen per person per night with dinner and breakfast included.

### Osaka (2 nights)

A short, high-energy finish. Osaka is Japan's best eating city for street food and casual dining, and the Namba/Dotonbori area delivers maximum atmosphere per hour.

**What to do:**
- **Dotonbori** — the neon-lit canal street. Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu, and everything else. Walk it at night.
- **Shinsaibashi** — covered shopping arcade, one of the longest in Japan.
- **Shinsekai** — retro neighborhood with kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers) joints and a Blade Runner atmosphere.
- **Nara day trip** (optional) — 45 minutes by Kintetsu Railway from Namba. Todai-ji Temple (giant Buddha), free-roaming deer in Nara Park, Kasuga Taisha shrine. A half-day trip (4-5 hours) fits if you go early on your full Osaka day. Skip it if the group is travel-fatigued after five nights in Shimoda plus a long transit day.
- **Final shopping block** — Shinsaibashi and Amerikamura for clothes, Den Den Town for electronics and anime.

**June note:** Osaka in June is hot (high 20s C), very humid, with rain. But Osaka's main attractions — food stalls, covered arcades, indoor markets — work well regardless of weather.

## Day-by-day

### Day 1: Arrive in Tokyo
- Check in, easy neighborhood walk
- Casual dinner near the hotel — don't fight jet lag with an ambitious plan

### Day 2: Tokyo — shared morning, split afternoon
- Coffee-and-walk morning together (Daikanyama/Nakameguro or Kiyosumi-Shirakawa)
- Shared lunch
- Afternoon split:
  - Shopping / vintage / cafe crawl (Shimokitazawa, Harajuku)
  - Gym / run / active outing
  - Free wandering
- One strong shared dinner

### Day 3: Tokyo — flexible day
- Morning free
- Optional zoo (Ueno) or aquarium (Tokyo Sea Life Park) branch
- Optional thrift/shopping run in Shimokitazawa
- Easy or splurge dinner

### Day 4: Train to Shimoda
- Mid-morning Saphir Odoriko from Tokyo Station (2h40m, no transfers)
- Arrive early afternoon, check in
- Walk the beach or Perry Road
- Relaxed dinner in town — try the kinmedai

### Day 5: Shared coast day
- One moderate scenic coastal walk — a cape or cliff route rather than multiple scattered stops
- Late lunch in town or by the coast
- Easy beach time in the late afternoon
- Low-key evening

### Day 6: Split beach and water day
- Track A: Beach morning at Shirahama or Tatadohama, relaxed lunch, downtime
- Track B: Surf lesson, boat outing, or another active coastal session
- Track C: Perry Road cafes, photography, slower coastal wandering
- Regroup for dinner

### Day 7: Day trip day
- Best option: drive to Dogashima for the sea cave boat tour + west coast scenery (~45 min each way)
- Alternative: Kawazu Seven Waterfalls hike (~30 min north)
- Alternative: premium slow day — onsen, good breakfast, beach, reading
- Good dinner

### Day 8: Flex / repeat day
- Weather and energy buffer
- Repeat the best thing from earlier: another beach day, second surf session, revisit a favorite spot
- Or: rental car scenic drive around the peninsula
- This day exists so you don't have to cram everything into fixed slots

### Day 9: Transfer to Osaka
- Mid-morning departure from Shimoda
- Private transfer to Mishima (~1.5 hrs) then Shinkansen to Shin-Osaka (~2 hrs), or train via Atami (~4.5 hrs total)
- Arrive late afternoon, check in
- Evening walk and dinner in Dotonbori

### Day 10: Osaka — full day
- Morning: coffee and wandering in Namba or Shinsekai
- Optional split:
  - Nara day trip (half day, 45 min each way by train)
  - Shopping in Shinsaibashi / Amerikamura
  - Active outing — run along the river, find a gym
- Fun final dinner together

### Day 11: Departure from Osaka
- Relaxed breakfast
- Short final shopping or coffee window
- Head to KIX for departure

## Key risks

1. **June rainy season (tsuyu)** is the biggest issue. Expect overcast skies, intermittent rain, and high humidity throughout. Beach days may get rained out. Have indoor backup plans (onsen, covered markets, day trip to Dogashima caves).
2. **Beach infrastructure not open** until mid-to-late July. Beaches are accessible but lifeguards, beach houses, and rental facilities won't be operating.
3. **Water temperature is cool** (20-22C / 68-72F). Fine with a wetsuit, but not warm enough for casual tropical swimming.
4. **Five nights in Shimoda is generous.** If weather is bad for 2-3 days, the group may feel restless. Consider the option of trimming to 3-4 nights.
5. **SFO-KIX nonstop** may not be available on your dates. Book early or plan for a one-stop connection.
6. **Luggage on trains:** Consider using takkyubin (luggage forwarding service) to ship bags from Tokyo to Shimoda and again to Osaka. Traveling with six people's luggage on local trains is painful.
