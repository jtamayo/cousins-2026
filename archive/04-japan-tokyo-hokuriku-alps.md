# Option 4: Tokyo + Hokuriku + Japanese Alps

The mountain-and-town route. Three nights in Tokyo, three nights in Kanazawa (with a Shirakawa-go day trip), two nights in Takayama for old-town atmosphere and a ryokan dinner, then two nights in Matsumoto with a Kamikochi hike. All surface transit — no flights inside Japan — moving through Japan's most photogenic interior.

## At a glance

- 11 days / 10 nights
- Tokyo (3N) → Kanazawa (3N) → Takayama (2N) → Matsumoto (2N) → depart via Tokyo
- 4 travel days, no domestic flights (all train and bus)
- Best for: town atmosphere, mountain scenery, hiking, photography, the most unusual repeat-Japan trip

## The honest case for this option

**Why pick it:** This is the route that feels least like a standard Japan trip. Instead of the Tokyo-Kyoto-Osaka corridor that most visitors follow, you move through regional cities and mountain towns that have their own distinct character. Kanazawa has world-class seafood, gold-leaf culture, and one of Japan's three great gardens. Takayama is a beautifully preserved Edo-era town with sake breweries and Hida beef. Kamikochi is a pristine mountain valley with crystal-clear rivers and views of the Northern Alps. The food across this entire route is superb — Kanazawa sushi, Takayama's morning markets, Matsumoto soba. If your group includes anyone who's been to Japan before and wants something different, or anyone who prioritizes photography and walking through atmospheric towns, this is the strongest option.

**Why not:** This route has more coordination friction than the others. You're visiting four bases in 11 days, moving by bus between Kanazawa-Takayama and Takayama-Matsumoto (the trains on these legs are impractical). Each transit segment is only 2-2.5 hours, which is very manageable, but there's less room for spontaneous split days in smaller towns where splitting means... walking in different directions for an hour. And notably, there's no Kyoto or Osaka on this itinerary — if anyone in the group has never been to Japan and dreams of Fushimi Inari and Kinkakuji, they may feel the absence.

## Getting there and back

**SFO → Tokyo:** Nonstop on United, ANA, JAL, Zipair. ~11 hours.

**Return from Matsumoto → Tokyo → SFO:** JR Azusa limited express from Matsumoto to Shinjuku, about 2.5-3 hours, comfortable and scenic. Then nonstop flight from NRT or HND to SFO. Alternatively, book an open-jaw ticket (into Tokyo, out of Osaka) and take the shinkansen from Kanazawa to Osaka instead of continuing east — but this changes the route shape significantly.

## How you move between sections

### Tokyo → Kanazawa (Day 4)

**Hokuriku Shinkansen (Kagayaki service):** about **2.5 hours**, direct, comfortable. Cost: ~14,000 yen reserved seat. Trains run 1-2 per hour.

This is an easy, fast, pleasant leg. Depart mid-morning, arrive by early afternoon, walk Omicho Market for a late lunch.

### Kanazawa → Takayama (Day 7)

**Direct highway bus (Nohi Bus / Hokutetsu Bus):** about **2 to 2.5 hours**. A few departures per day (3-4). Cost: ~3,500-4,000 yen.

This is the simplest option. **Book ahead for six seats** — these buses are limited capacity with limited departures. The alternative (train via Toyama, then JR Hida to Takayama) takes about the same time but costs more and requires a transfer.

**Pro tip:** Shirakawa-go is literally on the bus route between Kanazawa and Takayama. You can visit it as a stop en route instead of as a day trip from Kanazawa. This lets you reduce Kanazawa to 2 nights if you'd rather have an extra night elsewhere.

### Takayama → Matsumoto (Day 9)

**Direct bus (Nohi Bus / Alpico):** about **2 to 2.5 hours**. A few departures per day. Cost: ~3,100 yen.

**Book ahead for six.** The train alternative requires going via Nagoya — a massive 4+ hour detour. The bus is the only sensible option.

### Matsumoto → Tokyo (Day 11)

**JR Azusa limited express** to Shinjuku: about **2.5-3 hours**, roughly one per hour. Cost: ~6,600-7,000 yen. Comfortable ride through mountain scenery.

### Is there too much transit?

Four internal travel segments over 11 days. But each is only 2-3 hours — you lose a half-day on each, not the whole day. You arrive by early afternoon and still have time to explore. This is well within reason for Japan and honestly no worse than any other option with a domestic flight (which eats a similar half-day with airport time).

## Section by section

### Tokyo (3 nights)

Same landing zone as all options.

### Kanazawa (3 nights)

A polished regional city on the Sea of Japan coast, with one of Japan's best food scenes and a refined cultural identity. Kanazawa was never bombed in WWII, so its historic districts are authentic, not reconstructed.

**Core attractions:**
- **Kenrokuen Garden** — one of Japan's three great gardens. At its greenest and most lush in June (the moss and greenery peak during rainy season). Beautiful even — especially — in the rain. Allow 2-3 hours with adjacent Kanazawa Castle Park.
- **Higashi Chaya District** — a preserved geisha district with tea houses, gold-leaf shops, and traditional architecture. 1-2 hours of wandering.
- **Omicho Market** — a lively covered market with exceptional fresh seafood. Go in the morning for sushi breakfast or a seafood bowl. 1-2 hours.
- **21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art** — an excellent, often overlooked Kazuyo Sejima/SANAA-designed museum. Great for a rainy afternoon. 1-2 hours.
- **D.T. Suzuki Museum** — architecturally stunning and deeply peaceful. Named for the Buddhist philosopher. 1 hour.
- **Nagamachi Samurai District** — preserved samurai residences. Atmospheric. 1 hour.

**Is three nights too long?** Kanazawa's core can be seen in 1.5-2 full days. The third day needs to be intentional. Best option: **day trip to Shirakawa-go** — a UNESCO World Heritage village of thatched-roof farmhouses, about 1 hour 15 minutes by bus. This is a genuinely worthwhile excursion and the single best use of your third Kanazawa day. Alternatively, a half-day trip to **Kaga Onsen** (hot spring town, 30 min by train).

If you'd rather not add a day trip, consider trimming Kanazawa to 2 nights and stopping at Shirakawa-go on the bus ride to Takayama instead.

**Food:** Kanazawa's food scene is outstanding and may be the best of this entire itinerary. The Sea of Japan delivers extremely fresh seafood, and Kanazawa sushi rivals Tokyo at lower prices. Kaga cuisine (the regional kaiseki tradition) is refined and seasonal. Gold-leaf ice cream is the local sweet specialty.

### Takayama (2 nights)

A beautifully preserved Edo-era town in the mountains, with wooden merchant houses, sake breweries, morning markets, and Hida beef.

**What to do:**
- **Sanmachi Suji (old town)** — three parallel streets of dark-wood merchant houses, now housing sake breweries (with tastings), craft shops, and snack stalls. Compact — you can walk it thoroughly in 2-3 hours.
- **Morning markets** — Miyagawa Market (along the river) and Jinya-mae Market. Go early for local produce, pickles, and snacks.
- **Takayama Jinya** — a preserved Edo-era government building. Interesting and well-maintained. 45-60 min.
- **Hida Folk Village (Hida no Sato)** — open-air museum of traditional thatched-roof houses relocated from the surrounding mountains. 1-2 hours.
- **Shinhotaka Ropeway** (~70 min by bus from Takayama) — a two-stage gondola to 2,156m with stunning Northern Alps panoramas. Not strenuous (it's a cable car), but you can do short hikes at the top.
- **Higashiyama Walking Course** — a pleasant 3.5 km hillside temple walk on the eastern edge of town. Easy, 1-2 hours.
- **Cycling** — Takayama's surrounding countryside is flat and beautiful. Rice paddies, rural scenery, mountain backdrop.
- **Hida beef** — the local wagyu. Try it grilled, as sushi, or in a croquette from a street stall.

**Ryokan experience:** This is the best place on the itinerary for a ryokan night with kaiseki dinner. Honjin Hiranoya Kachoan (high-end, central), Tanabe Ryokan (traditional, well-reviewed), or Oyado Koto no Yume (boutique, central). Expect 25,000-50,000 yen per person per night with dinner and breakfast. **Book 2-3 months ahead** for three rooms at a good property.

**Two nights is the right amount.** One full day for the town, one for an excursion (Shinhotaka Ropeway or cycling).

### Matsumoto / Kamikochi (2 nights)

Matsumoto is a mid-sized castle town with a great craft beer scene and soba restaurants. Kamikochi is a pristine mountain valley in the Northern Alps, accessible only by bus (private cars are banned).

**Matsumoto itself:**
- **Matsumoto Castle** — one of only 12 original-construction castles remaining in Japan (most are concrete reconstructions). Black exterior, dramatic against the mountain backdrop. The hilltop gives excellent views. 2-3 hours including the walk up.
- **Nawate Street and Nakamachi Street** — old merchant streets with craft shops, cafes, and frog mascots.
- **Soba** — Matsumoto is in the heart of soba country. The local buckwheat noodles are the best in Japan.
- **Craft beer** — Matsumoto Brewery and several others.

**Kamikochi day trip (from Matsumoto base):**
- **Getting there:** Bus from Matsumoto to Shin-Shimashima station (Alpico Railway, ~30 min), then bus to Kamikochi (~65 min). Total: about 1.5-2 hours each way. Leave by 7-8 AM, return by late afternoon.
- **Private cars are NOT allowed** in Kamikochi. Bus or taxi from the transfer points only.
- **The experience:** A flat river valley at ~1,500m elevation surrounded by 3,000m peaks. Crystal-clear Azusa River, alpine meadows, old-growth forest. It is jaw-droppingly beautiful.
- **Main trails (all easy, suitable for mixed fitness):**
  - Kappa Bridge to Taisho Pond: flat boardwalk along the river, ~3.5 km one way (1 hour). Stunning.
  - Kappa Bridge to Myojin Pond: flat riverside path, ~3 km one way (1 hour). Serene.
  - Full loop combining both: ~10 km, 3-4 hours, flat the entire way. This is the classic Kamikochi day hike.
- **June conditions:** Trails are clear of snow on the valley floor. Some muddy sections from rain — bring waterproof hiking shoes. Cool: 10-20C (50-68F), so pack layers. Bring insect repellent and rain gear.
- **Make it opt-in:** Kamikochi requires 3-4 hours of bus time round trip. Non-hikers should stay in Matsumoto and enjoy the castle, soba shops, and craft beer instead. This is a natural group split point.

**Kamikochi weather risk:** Heavy rain can occasionally close trails or make the day unpleasant. Have Matsumoto Castle as a backup plan if the weather turns.

## Day-by-day

### Day 1: Arrive in Tokyo
- Check in, easy neighborhood walk, casual dinner

### Day 2: Tokyo — shared morning, split afternoon
- Coffee-and-walk morning (Daikanyama/Nakameguro or Kiyosumi-Shirakawa)
- Shared lunch
- Afternoon split: shopping / active outing / free wandering
- One strong shared dinner

### Day 3: Tokyo — flexible day
- Morning free
- Optional Shimokitazawa shopping, gym, or photo walk
- Easy dinner

### Day 4: Shinkansen to Kanazawa
- Hokuriku Shinkansen from Tokyo (~2.5 hrs)
- Arrive early afternoon
- Walk to Omicho Market for a late sushi lunch
- Easy evening walk around the Higashi Chaya district
- Dinner in a seafood izakaya

### Day 5: Shared Kanazawa day
- Morning: Kenrokuen Garden (lush and green in June, beautiful even in rain)
- Lunch near the garden or at a Kaga cuisine restaurant
- Afternoon: 21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art + Nagamachi Samurai District
- Evening: D.T. Suzuki Museum if it's still open, or free time
- Dinner together

### Day 6: Shirakawa-go day trip (or split Kanazawa day)
- **Option A:** Bus to Shirakawa-go (~1h15m each way). Walk the village, see the thatched-roof houses, observation deck for the panoramic view. Half to full day.
- **Option B:** Split day in Kanazawa — craft shopping, museum revisits, Kaga Onsen half-day trip, or a slower pace
- Dinner in Kanazawa

### Day 7: Bus to Takayama
- Highway bus from Kanazawa (~2-2.5 hrs). Book seats in advance for six.
- Alternative: stop at Shirakawa-go en route if you didn't go on Day 6
- Arrive Takayama early afternoon
- Walk Sanmachi Suji (old town), pop into sake breweries for tastings
- Easy dinner — try Hida beef

### Day 8: Takayama full day
- Morning: Miyagawa Morning Market, Takayama Jinya
- Split afternoon:
  - Track A: Shinhotaka Ropeway (~70 min bus each way, Northern Alps panorama)
  - Track B: Hida Folk Village + cycling in the countryside
  - Track C: Deeper old-town wandering, cafes, photography, onsen
- **Ryokan dinner** — kaiseki meal together at your inn. This is a trip highlight.

### Day 9: Bus to Matsumoto
- Highway bus from Takayama (~2-2.5 hrs). Book in advance for six.
- Arrive early afternoon
- Matsumoto Castle visit
- Wander Nawate Street and Nakamachi Street
- Soba dinner — the best in Japan

### Day 10: Kamikochi day (or Matsumoto day)
- **Hikers:** Early bus to Kamikochi (depart ~7-8 AM, arrive ~9:30 AM). Walk the Kappa Bridge → Taisho Pond → Myojin Pond loop (~10 km, 3-4 hrs, flat). Return by late afternoon.
- **Non-hikers:** Stay in Matsumoto. Revisit the castle, explore craft beer spots, shop, or take it easy.
- Everyone regroups for a final dinner together

### Day 11: Train to Tokyo, depart
- JR Azusa from Matsumoto to Shinjuku (~2.5-3 hrs)
- Connect to NRT/HND for SFO flight
- Keep this day clean — no sightseeing, just transit

## Key risks

1. **June rainy season (tsuyu)** affects the entire route. Kanazawa is one of Japan's rainiest cities year-round. Takayama and Matsumoto will get regular showers. Kamikochi in rain can be misty and muddy. **Pack proper rain gear** (jackets, not just umbrellas) and waterproof shoes. The upside: Kenrokuen garden is genuinely more beautiful in the rain, and the mountain mist adds atmosphere.
2. **Bus bookings for six people** on the Kanazawa→Takayama and Takayama→Matsumoto routes need to be done early. Limited seats, limited departures per day.
3. **No Kyoto/Osaka on this itinerary.** Make sure everyone is aligned that this is a "deeper Japan" trip, not a greatest-hits tour. First-timers dreaming of Fushimi Inari may feel the absence.
4. **Less split-day flexibility** than city-based options. Takayama and Kamikochi offer fewer independent options than Tokyo or Osaka. Build in opt-in/opt-out at Kamikochi.
5. **Ryokan rooms in Takayama** for three couples need to be booked 2-3 months ahead. Good properties fill up.
6. **Kamikochi can close in severe weather.** Have Matsumoto as a backup plan.

## Who should pick this

Choose this option if your group values atmosphere, food, and distinctive towns over beach time or big-city shopping. This is the most "insider Japan" trip of the five — Kanazawa's seafood, Takayama's old town, and Kamikochi's mountain valley are the kind of places that Japanese travelers themselves seek out. It requires the most group coordination but delivers the most distinctive memories.
