# Japan Trip Comparison

Six adults, three couples, 11 days in June. Five options. Here's how to think about the choice.

## The two-sentence version

If you want the **easiest trip to say yes to**, pick Tokyo + Izu + Osaka — it's the lowest-friction option with the best food and shopping, though June rain may undercut the beach days.

If you want the **most memorable and varied trip**, pick Tokyo + Setouchi + Shikoku — it has world-class art, a legendary cycling route, and Japan's oldest onsen, but requires more advance planning.

## Option by option

### 1. Tokyo + Izu + Osaka
**File:** `01-japan-tokyo-izu-kansai.md`

The safe pick. Shimoda has real beaches, good surf, and coastal walks. Tokyo and Osaka bookend the trip with the best food and shopping in Japan. Easy split days — couples can do their own thing and regroup for dinner.

- **Best for:** group consensus, food lovers, couples who want autonomy
- **Biggest risk:** June rainy season hits beach plans hardest here. Beach facilities don't open until mid-July. Five nights in Shimoda could feel long if weather is bad.
- **Key logistics:** No domestic flights. Shimoda→Osaka transfer (~4.5 hrs by train) is the longest move. SFO-KIX nonstop return has limited availability — book early.

### 2. Tokyo + Okinawa + Osaka
**File:** `02-japan-tokyo-okinawa.md`

The high-upside, high-risk pick. The only option with genuinely warm tropical water, snorkeling, and resort pool days. When late-June weather cooperates, it's spectacular.

- **Best for:** warm water, resort energy, relaxed pace
- **Biggest risk:** Most weather-sensitive option by far. Tsuyu typically ends ~June 20-23 but varies. Early/mid June could be multiple rainy days. Main island beaches underwhelm — the Kerama Islands (35-50 min ferry from Naha) are where the real water quality is.
- **Key logistics:** Two domestic flights (most of any option). Rental car mandatory on Okinawa main island. Recommend splitting: 3 nights main island + 2 nights Kerama Islands.

### 3. Tokyo + Hokkaido
**File:** `03-japan-tokyo-hokkaido.md`

The weather hedge. Hokkaido is the only part of Japan that doesn't get formal rainy season. June there means 20-23C days, minimal rain, and 15+ hours of daylight. Sapporo's food scene is world-class. The scenic base delivers hiking, rafting, onsen hopping, and mountain views.

- **Best for:** reliable weather, outdoor activities, food, photography
- **Biggest risk:** No warm water or beach component. Requires a rental car in rural Hokkaido. Departure means connecting through Tokyo (no direct US flights from Sapporo).
- **Key logistics:** One domestic flight. Recommend splitting the scenic base: Lake Toya (2N) + Niseko (3N) instead of 5 nights in one place. Vacation rental for the group is ideal and cheap in summer.

### 4. Tokyo + Hokuriku + Alps
**File:** `04-japan-tokyo-hokuriku-alps.md`

The insider route. Kanazawa's seafood rivals Tokyo. Takayama's old town is Edo-era Japan preserved in amber. Kamikochi's mountain valley is jaw-droppingly beautiful. The most unusual and photogenic itinerary — and the best food across the full trip.

- **Best for:** town atmosphere, mountain scenery, photography, food, repeat Japan visitors
- **Biggest risk:** Most coordination required. Bus segments (Kanazawa→Takayama, Takayama→Matsumoto) need advance booking for six. Less split-day freedom in smaller towns. No Kyoto/Osaka may disappoint first-timers.
- **Key logistics:** No domestic flights — all train and bus. Book buses early. Ryokan in Takayama needs 2-3 month advance booking for three rooms. Kamikochi is a day trip (bus only, no cars) — make it opt-in for non-hikers.

### 5. Tokyo + Setouchi + Shikoku
**File:** `05-japan-tokyo-setouchi-shikoku.md`

The variety pick. No two days feel the same — art islands, ferry-hopping, a world-famous cycling route, Japan's oldest onsen. The Chichu Art Museum and Teshima Art Museum are genuinely extraordinary. The Shimanami Kaido is a bucket-list bike ride.

- **Best for:** art, cycling, variety, active novelty without roughing it
- **Biggest risk:** Most advance planning required. Art island museums closed on Mondays (hard constraint on scheduling). Chichu Art Museum timed tickets and e-bike reservations for six must be booked early. Rain can disrupt the cycling day.
- **Key logistics:** One domestic flight (departure from Matsuyama). Plan arrival around Monday closures. Reserve e-bikes well in advance. Use takkyubin luggage forwarding at every base change.

## Decision questions

Before picking, the group should align on these:

1. **How much does guaranteed good weather matter?** If a lot → Hokkaido rises. If you'll roll with rain → more options open up.
2. **Is warm-water beach time a real priority or a nice-to-have?** If essential → Okinawa is the only option. If nice-to-have → Izu scratches the itch. If not needed → Hokkaido, Alps, or Setouchi.
3. **How much advance planning will you actually do?** Setouchi + Shikoku is the most rewarding but needs timed tickets, bike reservations, and calendar awareness. Izu + Kansai needs almost none.
4. **Is one domestic flight inside Japan acceptable?** If zero flights preferred → Izu + Kansai or Hokuriku + Alps. If flights are fine → all options open.
5. **Are you optimizing for safest group consensus or most memorable upside?** Consensus → Izu + Kansai. Upside → Setouchi + Shikoku or Hokkaido.
