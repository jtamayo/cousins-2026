# Trip B: Fire, Food, and the Hidden Islands
### The Kyushu Circuit — 12 days

![Steam rising over Beppu's volcanic hot springs, Kyushu](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_560,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1514407013/oita/Oita1991_1)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/kyushu/oita/)*

Three nights into this trip you are sitting in a private cedar-and-stone outdoor bath in a Kurokawa valley, steam rising from the water into the cold mountain air, surrounded by forest. The next morning you drive to the edge of one of the world's largest active calderas. A week later you are eating hand-stretched noodles boiled at your table in a remote Pacific island fishing village while planning the morning's sea kayak route to a Gothic church on a cliff. Kyushu is the Japan that experienced travelers haven't done, and it delivers something genuinely different at every stop.

---

## At a glance

- **12 days / 11 nights**
- Tokyo (2N) → Fukuoka (2N) → Kurokawa Onsen + Aso (2N) → Nagasaki (2N) → Goto Islands (3N)
- 1 domestic flight (Tokyo → Fukuoka), 1 car circuit, 1 jetfoil ferry (Nagasaki → Goto)
- **Best timing: Early-to-mid June** (June 1–20). This is Kyushu's rainy season, but the Kurokawa and Goto sections are rain-compatible — onsen in rain is wonderful, and the Goto sea conditions tend to be better in early June before typhoon swell season. If you must go late June, conditions are similar; it's slightly warmer and some days are clearer as tsuyu ends.
- This is the most weather-flexible itinerary of the three — rain enhances Kurokawa and doesn't significantly affect Goto, Nagasaki, or Fukuoka.

---

## What makes this trip compete with Turkey or Greece

The Goto Islands are the answer. They are a chain of subtropical Pacific islands with a deeply strange and beautiful history: hidden Christian communities that practiced their faith in secret for 250 years after Christianity was banned, building churches in remote fishing villages using techniques learned from Dutch traders, eventually emerging in the 1870s to construct Gothic and Romanesque brick churches that still hold active Sunday services. The islands have excellent snorkeling visibility (15-25 meters, better than most of the Mediterranean), resident bottlenose dolphin pods, handmade noodles unlike anything else in Japan, and almost no international tourists. Compare this to an afternoon in Cinque Terre.

Add Fukuoka — which many Japanese food obsessives consider the country's best eating city — and Kurokawa, which looks like a fairytale onsen village in a mountain valley, and you have a trip that's genuinely hard to replicate.

## Section by section

### Tokyo (2 nights)

Short and focused. The group has been here; these are jet lag days with personal agendas. Kappabashi kitchen street and Tsukiji outer market for the food-obsessed. Shimokitazawa or Koenji for vintage. One strong group dinner, booked ahead.

### Fukuoka (2 nights)

![Fukuoka cityscape — Japan's most livable city and its most underrated food destination](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_500,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1514400433/fukuoka/Fukuoka1619_8)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/kyushu/fukuoka/)*

Fukuoka's reputation as Japan's best food city is not contested by anyone who has eaten here. The yatai stalls along the Nakagawa riverbank set the tone on your first night: a dozen wooden tent-stalls, 8–10 people per counter, grilled skewers and cold beer under bare bulbs while the city walks past. From there: Hakata tonkotsu ramen at a counter at midnight, mentaiko (spicy cod roe) on everything, fresh tuna at Nagahama fish market before dawn, mizutaki chicken hot pot for the group. The Daimyo neighborhood has excellent independent boutiques and coffee. Ohori Park has a 5km lake loop for morning runs. Ramen Stadium on Canal City's top floor if you want to compare styles back to back. Everything is within 30 minutes of everything else.

### Kurokawa Onsen + Aso (2 nights)

![Mt. Aso — one of the world's largest active volcanic calderas, Kumamoto](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_500,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1514403676/kumamoto/Kumamoto1541_8)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/kyushu/kumamoto/)*

Drive 1.5 hours south from Fukuoka into the mountains of Kumamoto Prefecture. As the valley drops down, the ryokan appear through steam and cedar. This is what ryokan brochures promise and usually don't deliver: a small gorge town with 20-odd properties, each with its own outdoor baths, morning mist, the sound of water. The kaiseki dinner is 8–10 courses of local Kumamoto ingredients — river fish, venison, mountain vegetables — in the form of Japanese fine dining. Private kashikiri outdoor baths are available and recommended.

The next morning, the Aso caldera. What you're driving through on the approach IS the floor of a prehistoric supervolcano — farms, roads, and small towns all inside the collapsed caldera of an eruption so massive it destroyed an entire mountain. In the center rises a ring of five volcanic peaks. Kusasenri, a broad green meadow inside the caldera, has wild horses grazing against the volcanic backdrop. If the active Nakadake crater is accessible (alert levels vary — treat it as a bonus, not a certainty), you walk to the edge of a smoking volcanic cone above a turquoise acidic lake. If the crater is restricted, the Kuju mountain range offers a serious alpine traverse from 1,330m that earns views across all of Kyushu.

### Nagasaki (2 nights)

![Nagasaki harbor at night — one of Japan's most atmospheric port cities](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_500,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1516703891/nagasaki/Nagasaki2047_7)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/kyushu/nagasaki/)*

For 250 years, Nagasaki was the only port in Japan open to foreign trade. Everything Japan chose to know about the outside world — Dutch science, Chinese cooking, Portuguese baking — came through this harbor, filtered and controlled. The result is a city unlike anywhere else in Japan: hilly, harbor-wrapped, with a hybrid food culture that invented chanpon noodle soup, perfected castella sponge cake, and developed shippoku cuisine as a blend of all three traditions. The Atomic Bomb Museum is one of the best historical museums in Japan. Glover Garden's Victorian merchant mansions occupy a hillside with harbor views. The Mt. Inasa night panorama is one of Japan's three great city views. Two nights here passes too quickly.

### Goto Islands (3 nights)

A 1h25m jetfoil across the East China Sea, and you arrive at something genuinely unexpected. After Christianity was banned in Japan in 1597, communities of believers fled from mainland Kyushu to these remote islands, where they practiced their faith in complete secrecy for 250 years — hiding altars behind sliding screens, disguising the Virgin Mary as a Buddhist Kannon statue, passing everything by oral tradition with no priests and no books. When Christianity was legalized in 1873, they emerged to find their faith largely intact. They then built churches — Gothic, Romanesque, brick, stone — on the same islands where they had hidden. The UNESCO designation recognizes not just the buildings but the entire story.

Beyond the churches: resident Indo-Pacific bottlenose dolphins with encounter rates around 90%, guided sea kayak tours along coastline accessible only from the water, snorkeling and diving with 15–25 meters of visibility, and the Goto udon — hand-stretched noodles served jigoku-daki style, boiled at your table, dipped in cold flying-fish broth with raw egg. It is genuinely unlike any udon you have had before.

## Getting there and back

**SFO → Tokyo:** Nonstop on ANA, JAL, or United (~11 hrs) into Haneda.

**Tokyo → Fukuoka:** Domestic flight, Haneda to Fukuoka (FUK), ~1.5 hours. Multiple daily flights on ANA and JAL.

**Fukuoka → Kurokawa → Nagasaki:** Rent a car in Fukuoka and drive the circuit.
- Fukuoka to Kurokawa: ~1.5 hours by expressway
- Kurokawa to Aso (day trip): ~45-60 min drive
- Kurokawa to Nagasaki: ~1.5-2 hours by expressway via Kumamoto
- Return car at Nagasaki; pick up separate cars on Goto Island for the island section

**Nagasaki → Goto Islands (Fukue):** Jetfoil (Kyushu Shosen) from Nagasaki Port to Fukue, ~1 hour 25 minutes. About 3-4 departures per day. **Book in advance for 6 people** — the jetfoil has limited seats.

**Departure from Japan:** Fly from Nagasaki Airport (NGS) → Haneda (~1h40m) → SFO nonstop. Or take the Kamome limited express from Nagasaki to Hakata/Fukuoka (~2h) and fly from Fukuoka. Either works; Nagasaki Airport is convenient if you're starting the departure from there.

## Day-by-day

### Day 1: Arrive Tokyo
Land at Haneda, check in, easy dinner.

### Day 2: Tokyo — focused
Morning: Juan to Kappabashi or Tsukiji. Steph to Shimokitazawa.
Afternoon: Eliza shopping, Phil + Christian at large.
Evening: Strong group dinner, booked ahead.

### Day 3: Fly to Fukuoka
Fly Haneda → Fukuoka (~1.5 hrs). Pick up rental car(s) at Fukuoka Airport.
Check in. Afternoon/evening: Ohori Park walk or Fukuoka Castle ruins.
**Dinner: yatai stalls on Nakagawa riverbank.** This is the welcome-to-Fukuoka meal.

### Day 4: Fukuoka full day
Morning: Nagahama fish market at 6-7am (optional for early risers; Juan and Phil). Then Ohori Park run for the active set.
Late morning/afternoon: Daimyo neighborhood shopping (Steph, Eliza). Canal City if needed.
Late afternoon: Ohori Park coffee circuit.
**Dinner: Tonkotsu ramen deep dive** — two different shops. Or book a mizutaki hot pot restaurant for the group.

### Day 5: Drive to Kurokawa Onsen
Morning: One final Fukuoka breakfast (the convenience store onigiri here is also unironically excellent).
Drive to Kurokawa (~1.5 hrs). Check in, change, do nothing for an hour.
Afternoon: Walk the onsen town lanes. Private kashikiri bath booking for Juan & Steph. Others can use the nyuto tegata to visit 3 different ryokan's outdoor baths (¥1,300 passport).
**Evening: Full kaiseki dinner at the ryokan.** 8-10 courses. This is a meal to eat slowly.

### Day 6: Aso caldera
Split day based on crater access and fitness:
- **Crater open (Level 1 alert):** Drive to Aso. Everyone goes to Kusasenri meadow and Daikanbo viewpoint (1-2 hours, scenic, easy). Phil (and Christian, Juan, Steph) continue to Nakadake crater rim on foot or by road. Eliza and Lillian explore Kusasenri with the horses, drive the caldera rim.
- **Crater closed (Level 2 or higher):** Phil (and anyone fit) drives to Kuju range, hikes Makihatayama → Kujusan summit (4-5 hours). Eliza and Lillian do the Aso caldera scenic drive, Kusasenri meadow, horse country, Daikanbo. Regroup at Kurokawa for evening.
**Return to Kurokawa for the second kaiseki dinner.** Do not skip.

### Day 7: Drive to Nagasaki
Morning: Final Kurokawa morning. Walk the town before check-out.
Drive to Nagasaki (~1.5-2 hours). Check in.
Afternoon: Mt. Inasa cable car for the panoramic harbor view (do this while it's still light to understand the city layout). Then descend and walk to Hollander Slope.
**Evening: Mt. Inasa night view** (go back up, or find a different viewpoint). Dinner in Chinatown — chanpon, sara udon, cold Tsingtao.

### Day 8: Nagasaki full day
Morning: Atomic Bomb Museum + Peace Park. 2-3 hours. Take your time.
Midday: Dejima. Then lunch in Chinatown area.
Afternoon: Glover Garden. Walk the hillside neighborhoods.
Evening: **Shippoku dinner** — book ahead, this is a sit-down multi-course restaurant experience. The most distinctively Nagasaki meal. Castella shopping on the way back (Fukusaya).

### Day 9: Travel to Goto Islands
Morning: Return rental car in Nagasaki (or drop at the port-adjacent lot).
Jetfoil from Nagasaki Port to Fukue (~1h25m). Depart around 10-11am if possible; arrive before lunch.
Pick up rental cars at Fukue Port (2 cars, booked ahead).
**First stop: Goto udon lunch** at a port-area restaurant. Order the jigoku-daki. This is the meal everyone will reference for years.
Afternoon: Dozaki Church, coastal drive, check in to accommodation.
Evening: Simple island dinner at the guesthouse.

### Day 10: Church circuit and dolphins
Morning: **Dolphin tour** from Fukue port (book ahead, departs ~9am, ~1.5 hrs).
Midday: Drive to more churches on Fukue's south and west coast. Mizunoura Church.
Afternoon: Free. Swim, walk, rest.
Evening: Cook or guesthouse dinner.

### Day 11: Sea kayaking and offshore island
Morning: **Sea kayak tour** with local operator (~3-4 hours along Fukue's coast and sea caves). For non-kayakers: drive to southern viewpoints or cycle.
Afternoon: Day ferry to Nakadori Island (check schedule at Fukue Port). **Kashiragashima Church** on its clifftop promontory over the Pacific. Return to Fukue by evening.
**Final dinner:** The best Goto udon of the trip.

### Day 12: Fly home
Morning ferry or early arrangement back to the mainland (or fly directly from Fukue Airport to Nagasaki, ~30 min on a small ORC turboprop plane, then connect).
From Nagasaki: fly NGS → HND (~1h40m), connect to SFO nonstop.
Or: jetfoil back to Nagasaki, shinkansen Nagasaki → Hakata (~2h), fly Fukuoka → Haneda.
Allow 3+ hours for the international connection.

## What to book before you leave

In approximate order of urgency:
1. **Kurokawa ryokan** (3 rooms, private bath access, confirm tattoo policy) — book 2-3 months ahead for June weekends; Sanga, Yamamizuki, or Yamabiko
2. **Goto Islands accommodation** — 3 rooms at a guesthouse OR a house rental for the group; email Goto City Tourism for current options, or search Vacation Stay Japan / Airbnb
3. **Jetfoil tickets** — Nagasaki → Fukue, Kyushu Shosen; 6 seats, advance reservation required
4. **Goto rental cars** — 2 compact cars, Fukue Port area; small fleet, book ahead
5. **Goto dolphin tour** — 6 people simultaneously, call the operator directly
6. **Goto sea kayak tour** — small operator, 6 people needs specific confirmation
7. **Fukuoka car rental** — 1 large van or 2 compact cars, picked up at airport Day 3
8. **Shippoku dinner in Nagasaki** — a proper shippoku restaurant, reserve for 6
9. **International Driving Permits** for drivers (AAA, before departure)
10. **Check JMA Aso alert level** ~2 weeks before the trip; have Kuju range hike planned as the ready alternative

## Key risks

**Aso crater access is unpredictable.** Do not plan the trip around the crater being open. Plan around Kusasenri meadow and caldera views (always accessible) and treat the crater as a bonus. The Kuju hike is planned and ready as the alternative, which is arguably a better hiking experience for Phil anyway.

**Goto small-boat tours can be cancelled** in rough weather. The 3-night Fukue stay gives a buffer — if Day 10's dolphin tour is cancelled, it can shift to Day 11. If both are rough, take it as an extra church/cycling/snorkeling day. June before the main typhoon season is generally good for sea conditions, but build flexibility.

**Kurokawa is genuinely in demand for June.** Weekend nights in particular book 2-3 months out. If weeknights are possible, they're easier to secure and cheaper. Confirm tattoo/private bath policy explicitly at booking.

**Lillian and onsen:** She genuinely cannot use hot spring baths in pregnancy. This means she misses part of what makes Kurokawa special. Consider: (a) book a room type with a private attached outdoor bath and she can soak briefly at lower temperature with doctor clearance, or (b) frame Kurokawa as a spectacular dinner and atmosphere stay where she happens not to be in the water. The kaiseki dinner and the ryokan setting are valuable without the bath.

**Vegetarian on Goto:** The islands are a seafood culture. If Steph is strictly vegetarian (no seafood), she'll need to navigate carefully on Goto. Goto udon itself is vegetarian-compatible (the broth contains flying fish; if that's acceptable, it's fine). Rice, vegetables, and tofu dishes exist. The group accommodation with a kitchen helps. This is a minor friction, not a trip-breaker.

**Departure logistics from the Goto Islands.** The return journey (Fukue → Nagasaki → Haneda → SFO or Fukue → Nagasaki Airport → Haneda → SFO) requires an early start on Day 12. Plan for it. Don't book a 6am jetfoil on the last day unless you have an early flight that genuinely requires it.
