# Option 2: Tokyo + Okinawa + Osaka

The beach fantasy. Three nights in Tokyo, five nights in tropical Okinawa for resort life and warm water, then two nights in Osaka to close out with street food and shopping. This is the only option that delivers genuine warm-ocean swimming and snorkeling — but it's also the most weather-dependent and flight-heavy.

## At a glance

- 11 days / 10 nights
- Tokyo (3N) → Okinawa (5N) → Osaka (2N)
- 4 travel days, 2 domestic flights inside Japan
- Best for: warm water, resort energy, couples who want pool-and-beach days

## The honest case for this option

**Why pick it:** If warm-water beach time is a genuine top priority for your group, this is the only itinerary that delivers it. None of the other four options offer tropical swimming, snorkeling with sea turtles, or resort pool days. The daily rhythm is the simplest of all five options — wake up, go to beach or pool, eat well, repeat. For three couples who might struggle to coordinate city sightseeing, this relaxed pace is underrated. And when the weather cooperates, late-June Okinawa is spectacular: hot, clear, turquoise water, long days.

**Why not:** This is the highest-variance option. June is rainy season in Okinawa (tsuyu typically ends around June 20-23, but varies by year). If you travel in early-to-mid June, you could get multiple consecutive rainy days that gut the beach portion. You're also adding two domestic flights (Tokyo→Okinawa, Okinawa→Osaka), which means more airport time than any other option. The main island's beaches are pleasant but not spectacular by tropical standards — the really stunning water is at the outlying Kerama Islands, which adds another transit layer. And Okinawa, while culturally fascinating, delivers a more resort/tropical experience that you could arguably get elsewhere, rather than the distinctively Japanese content the other options offer.

## Getting there and back

**SFO → Tokyo:** Nonstop on United, ANA, JAL, Zipair. ~11 hours. Fly into Haneda if possible.

**Osaka → SFO:** United nonstop from KIX (book early, limited frequency). Fallback: domestic flight KIX→NRT, then nonstop to SFO.

## How you move between sections

### Tokyo → Okinawa (Day 4)

Domestic flight, Haneda to Naha. About **2.5 hours**. This is one of Japan's busiest domestic routes — 20-30+ flights per day across ANA, JAL, Skymark, Peach, and Jetstar. No trouble finding seats.

Cost: Budget carriers (Peach, Jetstar) can be 5,000-10,000 yen (~$35-70) booked early. ANA/JAL full-service 15,000-30,000 yen. Both ANA and JAL offer special domestic fares for international visitors (ANA "Experience Japan," JAL "Japan Explorer Pass") at 5,500-11,000 yen per segment — excellent value.

### Okinawa → Osaka (Day 9)

Domestic flight, Naha to Osaka Itami (closer to central Osaka) or KIX. About **2 hours**. Multiple daily flights on ANA, JAL, Peach, Jetstar, Skymark. Similar pricing to the Tokyo-Okinawa leg.

### Airport time budget

You have four airport experiences total: arrive Tokyo, fly to Okinawa, fly to Osaka, depart Osaka. Two of those are international legs you'd have on any itinerary. The two domestic flights each cost roughly a half-day (morning at hotel, get to airport, fly, arrive, get to new hotel, settle in by afternoon/evening). Japanese domestic airports are fast and efficient. Noticeable overhead, but manageable.

## Section by section

### Tokyo (3 nights)

Same as all options — your landing zone.

**Neighborhoods to explore:**
- **Daikanyama / Nakameguro** — boutiques, cafes, tree-lined canal
- **Shimokitazawa** — vintage, thrift, small bars
- **Kiyosumi-Shirakawa** — specialty coffee and a beautiful garden

**Activities:** Ueno Zoo, Tokyo Sea Life Park, one strong shared dinner night.

### Okinawa (5 nights)

The core of this trip. Okinawa is a subtropical island chain ~1,600 km south of Tokyo with its own distinct culture (Ryukyu Kingdom heritage), cuisine, and atmosphere.

**The beach reality check:** People who hear "Okinawa" often imagine Maldives-level turquoise water. The main island's beaches are pleasant but not spectacular by tropical standards. Popular beaches (Manza, Araha, Sunset Beach in Chatan) have decent sand but are often lined with seawalls and concrete wave-breakers. The truly stunning water and coral are at the **Kerama Islands** — a 35-50 minute high-speed ferry ride from Naha.

**Strong recommendation: split your five nights.** Instead of staying on the main island the whole time, do:
- **3 nights** at a resort on the main island's west coast (Onna Village, Chatan, or Yomitan area)
- **2 nights** on **Zamami** or **Tokashiki** in the Kerama Islands

The Keramas have Japan's best beach water — crystal clear, sea turtles are common, coral gardens are healthy. Zamami's Furuzamami Beach and Tokashiki's Aharen Beach are consistently ranked among Japan's best. This split is what makes the Okinawa option genuinely worth choosing.

**Main island activities:**
- **Churaumi Aquarium** — on the Motobu Peninsula in the far north. About 1.5-2 hours by car from resort areas. The Kuroshio Sea tank with whale sharks and manta rays is genuinely world-class. Combine with a northern coast drive. Good rainy-day option.
- **Resort days** — pool, spa, cocktails. The main island has excellent resort infrastructure.
- **Scenic coast drive** to Cape Manzamo and northern viewpoints
- **Ryukyu culture** — Shuri Castle (rebuilt after the 2019 fire, partially reopened), Kokusai Street in Naha for shopping and awamori (Okinawan spirit)

**Kerama Islands activities:**
- **Snorkeling with sea turtles** — Zamami and Tokashiki have reliable turtle encounters in shallow, clear water
- **Beach days** — the real postcard beaches are here
- **Kayaking** through mangroves and coastal waters
- **Diving** — if anyone in the group dives, Kerama visibility (15-30m at good sites) is world-class by Japan standards

**Surfing note:** Okinawa is not a strong surf destination. The reefs block most swell and waves are generally small and inconsistent, especially pre-typhoon season in June. Stand-up paddleboarding and kayaking are more reliable water activities. Adjust expectations or plan SUP instead.

**You need a rental car on the main island.** Public transit outside Naha is limited (there's a monorail in Naha only, buses are slow and infrequent). For six people, rent a large van or two cars. International Driving Permit required — get this from AAA before you leave.

**Where to stay (main island):**
- **Splurge tier:** Halekulani Okinawa (Onna Village — sister to the Waikiki original, outstanding), Hoshinoya Okinawa (Yomitan — minimalist luxury), The Ritz-Carlton Okinawa (Nago), Hyatt Regency Seragaki Island
- **Upper-mid:** Renaissance Okinawa, Sheraton Okinawa Sunmarina, Kafuu Resort Fuchaku
- For the Kerama nights, accommodation is simpler — guesthouses, minshuku, and small pensions on Zamami or Tokashiki. Book early as options are limited.

### Osaka (2 nights)

Same finishing role as Option 1. Dotonbori, Shinsaibashi, street food, optional Nara day trip.

## Key risks

1. **Rainy season is the make-or-break factor.** Okinawa's tsuyu typically ends June 20-23 but varies year to year (can linger into early July). Early-to-mid June is squarely in rainy season — expect heavy rain, overcast skies, high humidity. Late June (post-tsuyu) is when the trip really sings. If you can't travel in the last week of June, seriously consider a different option.
2. **Main island beaches underwhelm vs. expectations.** Without the Kerama Islands detour, five nights on the main island will feel like padding. The Kerama split is what saves this itinerary.
3. **Two domestic flights** make this the most transit-heavy option. Each costs roughly a half-day.
4. **Rental car is mandatory.** Budget for it, get International Driving Permits, and plan for parking.
5. **Typhoon risk is low but not zero in June.** Main typhoon season is July-October, but June typhoons occasionally approach Okinawa. A direct hit would wreck the beach portion.
6. **Surf expectations should be low.** Plan for SUP/kayaking instead.
7. **KIX-SFO nonstop** has limited frequency. Book early for six seats.

## Who should pick this

Choose this option if warm-water beach time is a genuine non-negotiable for your group and you can travel in the **last week of June**. Accept that you're trading weather certainty and "classic Japan" content for the highest possible beach upside. If the weather cooperates, this will be the most relaxing trip of the five. If it doesn't, it will be the most frustrating.
