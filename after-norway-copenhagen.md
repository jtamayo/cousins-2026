# Copenhagen — After Norway

**2–3 nights · June 19–22ish**
For J&S (and maybe C&E if they want to overlap before heading home)

---

## Why Copenhagen

- Direct flights from Bergen (SAS/Norwegian, ~1.5h) and from Alesund (via Oslo, ~3–4h) — easy connection from any itinerary
- Walkable, bikeable, compact — a good reset after driving through fjords for 10 days
- Excellent vegetarian food scene (one of the best in Europe)
- Great running routes along the harbors and through parks
- Thrift shopping and vintage stores, especially in Norrebro and Vesterbro
- Coffee culture that rivals any city in Scandinavia
- Not too far from the next leg of J&S's trip (easy flights to Istanbul, other Scandinavian cities, or elsewhere in Europe)

## Events during the window

**Sankt Hans Aften (June 23)** — Denmark's midsummer bonfire night. At sunset, bonfires are lit on beaches and in parks across the country, with communal singing, witch effigies, eating and drinking outdoors. It's one of Denmark's biggest communal celebrations and the whole city turns out. If C&E are in Copenhagen on the 23rd, this is a genuine cultural highlight — exactly the kind of "something happening" that Christian gravitates toward.

Note: Norway celebrates the same night as **Jonsok** with similar bonfires, especially in western Norway. If the shared itinerary runs through Jun 23, the group could catch Jonsok in Bergen, Alesund, or wherever they are. But since the split happens Jun 19, only the post-split destinations matter for this.

## What to do

### Food & drink
- **Torvehallerne** — covered food market, good for browsing and grazing
- **Reffen** — street food market on the waterfront (seasonal, check if open)
- Strong vegetarian/vegan restaurant scene: Ark, Souls, SimpleRaw, and many regular restaurants with good veggie options
- Coffee: The Coffee Collective, Prolog, Democratic Coffee — Copenhagen takes coffee seriously

### Neighborhoods
- **Nyhavn** — the iconic colorful harbor. Touristy but genuinely beautiful, especially in the evening
- **Norrebro** — diverse, trendy, great for thrift shopping and people-watching. Assistens Cemetery (where Kierkegaard is buried) is a popular park for locals
- **Vesterbro** — former meatpacking district turned hip. Good vintage shops, bars, restaurants
- **Christiania** — the "free town." Polarizing but culturally interesting. Worth walking through once

### Outdoors & sights
- **Bike everywhere** — Copenhagen is one of the most bike-friendly cities in the world. Rent bikes and use them as primary transport
- **Harbor swimming** — Copenhagen has clean-water harbor baths (Islands Brygge, Fisketorvet). June water temp is brisk but swimmable
- **Kastellet & The Little Mermaid** — star-shaped fortress and the famous statue. Nice for a morning run along the waterfront
- **Louisiana Museum of Modern Art** — 35 min north by train, on the coast. World-class art in a beautiful setting. Worth a half day
- **Tivoli Gardens** — historic amusement park in the city center. Charming in the evening

### Day trips (if you have 3 nights)
- **Malmo, Sweden** — 35 min by train across the Oresund Bridge. Different vibe, good fika culture, Turning Torso tower
- **Roskilde** — Viking Ship Museum, 30 min by train

## Practical notes

- **Getting there from Norway**: Bergen → Copenhagen is ~1.5h direct (SAS, Norwegian). Book early for best fares
- **Getting around**: Walk or rent bikes. The Metro is good but you won't need it much in the center
- **Cost**: Copenhagen is expensive (similar to Norway). Budget ~$200–300/night for a decent hotel for two, meals ~$20–40/person
- **Currency**: Danish Krone (DKK), but cards accepted literally everywhere
- **J&S onward**: Copenhagen has direct flights to Istanbul (~3.5h), Stockholm (~1.5h), Amsterdam (~1.5h), and most of Europe. Good launchpad for the next leg
