# Option A — Out and Back from Bergen

**Bergen (2N) → Sognefjord / Flam area (3N) → Hardangerfjord area (3N) → Bergen (1N)**
Shared days: ~9 nights, June 9–18

---

## The idea

Everyone flies into Bergen. You use Bergen as your anchor and explore Norway's two most famous fjord systems — Sognefjord (the longest and deepest) and Hardangerfjord (the "orchard fjord") — as a loop. You return to Bergen around June 18, and the group splits:

- **P&L** fly Bergen → Bodo → Lofoten for their adventure week
- **C&E** head to a second European city (Oslo, Copenhagen, Amsterdam, or Stockholm) for a few more days before flying home ~June 25
- **J&S** continue their European trip — could overlap with C&E's city stop or head in their own direction

### Why this shape works

- Bergen has the most direct flights from the US (via London, Amsterdam, Copenhagen) — shortest total travel time for the group
- Bergen → Bodo has multiple daily flights and is the easiest gateway to Lofoten for P&L
- Returning to Bergen before splitting means nobody has to backtrack or rebook from a random fjord town
- The loop shape means you're never doing the same drive/ferry twice

## Day-by-day sketch

### Days 1–2: Bergen (June 9–11)

- Arrive, recover from jetlag
- Bryggen wharf, fish market, Floibanen funicular
- Mt. Floyen or Mt. Ulriken hike (easy to moderate, great city views)
- Coffee, shopping, and dinner in the city center
- Bergen has excellent vegetarian restaurants and a strong food scene

### Days 3–5: Sognefjord / Flam area (June 11–14)

- Drive or take the "Norway in a Nutshell" route to Flam
- **Flam Railway** — one of the world's most scenic train rides
- **Aurlandsfjord kayaking** — calm water, towering walls
- **Stegastein viewpoint** — dramatic fjord overlook, good for photos
- Hike options: Rimstigen (challenging, 4–5h, jaw-dropping views) or easier walks along the fjord
- Possible day trip to Nærøyfjord (UNESCO) by boat
- **Nigardsbreen glacier hike** (day trip, ~1.5h drive from Sogndal) — guided walks on a blue-ice glacier arm of Jostedalsbreen, Europe's largest glacier. Options from 3.5h (45 min on ice, NOK 620) to 6h with ice climbing (NOK 1,415). Ages 8–12+ depending on route. A genuine "nowhere else" experience. [bfl.no](https://www.bfl.no/en/nigardsbreen-glacier-hikes/)
- Base in Flam, Aurland, or Laerdal

### Days 6–8: Hardangerfjord area (June 14–17)

- Drive south to the Hardangerfjord region
- **Trolltunga** hike (the iconic rock shelf photo — 10–12h, very demanding) or shorter alternatives like the Bondhus glacier lake trail
- **Hardangervidda** — Norway's largest national park, open plateau hikes
- **Voss** — adventure sports capital (paragliding, rafting, zipline)
- Fruit orchards in bloom, cider tastings
- Option for a fjord cruise on Hardangerfjord

### Day 9: Return to Bergen (June 17–18)

- Drive back to Bergen (~2.5h from Hardangerfjord)
- Last group dinner
- Pack and prep for the split

### June 18: Group splits

- P&L: fly Bergen → Bodo, ferry/bus to Lofoten
- C&E: fly Bergen → city of choice, or stay one extra night
- J&S: continue onward

---

## Strengths

- Simplest logistics — one arrival airport, return to the same city
- Bergen is the most accessible Norwegian city from the US
- Best Lofoten connection for P&L (Bergen → Bodo is a major route)
- No wasted travel days — the loop is efficient
- Plenty of "easy day" alternatives alongside big hikes

## Tradeoffs

- You don't see Geirangerfjord or the Alesund coast
- Sognefjord and Hardangerfjord are the most popular fjord areas — more tourists than the route between Alesund and Bergen
- The group has to return to Bergen before splitting, which uses ~half a day

---

## Rough cost signals

- Flights SFO → Bergen: typically $800–1,200 RT via European hubs
- Accommodation: Bergen hotels $150–250/night; fjord-area lodges and cabins $120–300/night
- Car rental (2 cars for 6 people): ~$80–120/day per car
- P&L flights Bergen → Bodo: ~$100–200 one way
