# Option D — Stavanger to Bergen

**Stavanger (2N) → Lysefjord / Preikestolen (2N) → Hardangerfjord (3N) → Bergen (2N)**
Shared days: ~9 nights, June 9–18

---

## The idea

Fly into Stavanger and start with Norway's most dramatic cliff hikes — Preikestolen and possibly Kjeragbolten — then drive north through the Hardangerfjord region (Trolltunga, Voss, glacier lakes) and end in Bergen for the split. This is the hiking-heavy option: you get two of Norway's three famous cliff formations (Preikestolen and Trolltunga) in one trip.

- **P&L** fly Bergen → Bodo → Lofoten (easy, multiple daily flights)
- **C&E** fly Bergen → their next city (excellent connections)
- **J&S** continue from Bergen

### Why this shape works

- Stavanger has good European hub connections (London, Amsterdam, Copenhagen) — similar ease to Bergen
- You get Lysefjord AND Hardangerfjord — two different fjord systems with different character
- Preikestolen is a shorter, more accessible iconic hike than Trolltunga, so the group can ease into it
- Ends in Bergen for the best splitting logistics (same advantage as Options A and C)
- The route goes south-to-north with no backtracking

---

## Day-by-day sketch

### Days 1–2: Stavanger (June 9–11)

- Arrive, recover from jetlag
- Old Stavanger (Gamle Stavanger) — cobblestone streets, white wooden houses
- Norwegian Petroleum Museum — surprisingly interesting, tells the story of Norway's oil wealth
- Ovre Holmegate — Stavanger's colorful street, good for coffee and shopping
- Strong food scene, especially seafood; good vegetarian options
- Easy first day to settle in

### Days 3–4: Lysefjord / Preikestolen area (June 11–13)

- Base near Jorpeland or at a fjord lodge
- **Preikestolen (Pulpit Rock)** — the iconic flat-topped cliff 604m above Lysefjord. 4h round trip, moderate difficulty. One of the most photographed spots in Norway
- **Lysefjord cruise** — see Preikestolen from below, waterfalls, remote farms
- **Kjeragbolten** (optional, long day) — the famous boulder wedged 1,000m above the fjord. 6–8h round trip, more demanding. Not for everyone, but Phil and Christian would probably love it
- **Flørli 4444** — hike up the world's longest wooden staircase (4,444 steps), accessible by ferry only
- Easier alternative: fjord kayaking on Lysefjord

### Days 5–7: Hardangerfjord area (June 13–16)

- Drive north (~3.5h) to the Hardangerfjord region
- **Trolltunga** — the rock shelf jutting out over a 700m drop. 10–12h, very demanding. The big one. Not mandatory — plenty of alternatives for those who prefer a shorter day
- **Bondhus glacier lake** — shorter hike to a turquoise lake beneath the Folgefonna glacier (2–3h)
- **Voss** — Norway's adventure sports capital: rafting, paragliding, zipline
- **Hardangervidda National Park** — Norway's largest, open high plateau, easier walking
- Fruit orchards along the fjord (June is blossom season), cider tasting
- Possible fjord cruise on Hardangerfjord

### Days 8–9: Bergen (June 16–18)

- Drive to Bergen (~2.5h from Hardangerfjord)
- Bryggen wharf, fish market, Floibanen funicular
- Mt. Floyen or Ulriken hike
- Shopping, coffee, exploring the city
- Final group dinners — Bergen has the best restaurant scene on this route

### June 18: Group splits

- P&L: fly Bergen → Bodo, then to Lofoten
- C&E: fly Bergen → onward
- J&S: continue from Bergen

## Strengths

- Best hiking of any option — Preikestolen AND Trolltunga in one trip
- Preikestolen is shorter and more accessible (good warm-up before Trolltunga)
- Lysefjord is less touristy than Sognefjord
- Kjeragbolten and Flørli 4444 are unique additions not available on other routes
- Ends in Bergen — best splitting logistics for everyone
- Two distinct fjord systems (Lysefjord + Hardangerfjord)

## Tradeoffs

- You skip Sognefjord, Flam Railway, and Nærøyfjord (the classic "Norway in a Nutshell" experiences)
- No Geirangerfjord or Alesund
- More physically demanding overall — two big-ticket hikes might be too much for some
- Slightly more driving than Option A
- Stavanger → Hardangerfjord drive is ~3.5h, which is a heavier travel day

## Rough cost signals

- Flights SFO → Stavanger: $800–1,300 via European hubs
- Accommodation: Stavanger hotels $150–250/night; fjord lodges $120–300/night
- Car rental (2 cars, one-way with drop-off): ~$100–150/day per car
- P&L flights Bergen → Bodo: ~$100–200
