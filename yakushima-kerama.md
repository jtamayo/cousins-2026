# Trip A: Ancient Forests and Turtle Beaches
### Yakushima + Kerama Islands — 12 days

![Ancient Yakushima cedar forest — Jomon Sugi, one of the world's oldest trees](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_560,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1514404696/kagoshima/Kagoshima1783_3)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/kyushu/kagoshima/yakushima/)*

This is the "something extraordinary for everyone" trip. You spend four nights on a UNESCO island so ancient and atmospheric it inspired a Studio Ghibli film, then three nights on a string of small islands in the East China Sea with the clearest water in Japan. No temples, no bullet trains, no cities — this is Japan at its most primal.

---

## At a glance

- **12 days / 11 nights**
- Tokyo (3N) → Yakushima (4N) → Kerama Islands (3N) → Naha departure
- 2 domestic flights, 2 ferries
- **Best timing:** June 20–30 (post-tsuyu for Kerama; June is deep in sea turtle nesting season)
- If you travel June 10–20: Yakushima is fine, Kerama is wetter but still viable
- **Not for early June** if warm dry Kerama water is a priority

---

## What makes this trip compete with Greece or Sicily

This trip isn't competing with beach resorts. It's competing with the feeling of going somewhere genuinely remote and encountering things unavailable anywhere else. On Yakushima, you walk through a forest where individual trees are older than the Roman Empire, while wild primates watch you from the branches. On Kerama, sea turtles the size of coffee tables graze coral in 8 meters of clear water directly below your snorkel. Neither of those things is easy to replicate elsewhere. The comparison to Galápagos isn't an exaggeration.

## Section by section

### Tokyo (3 nights)

Three nights of jet lag management and personal agendas — the group has been here before, so this is not a highlights tour. Morning coffee crawl through Kiyosumi-Shirakawa, one of Tokyo's most concentrated specialty coffee neighborhoods. Shimokitazawa for vintage clothing and independent records. Kappabashi kitchen street for those with a knife and ceramic obsession. One strong group dinner, booked in advance. Tokyo is still the best restaurant city in the world, and a great first night sets the tone.

### Yakushima (4 nights)

Yakushima is a near-circular island 25km across, with peaks over 1,900 meters in its interior. It receives 8,000–10,000mm of rainfall annually in its mountain zones — one of the wettest places in East Asia, and precisely why the cedars grow the way they do. The ancient Yakusugi cedars, any tree over 1,000 years old on the island, are among the oldest living organisms in Japan.

You stay at **Sankara Hotel & Spa** — three private forest villas set into a hillside, each with its own outdoor bath, and a restaurant drawing from Yakushima's remarkable pantry: flying fish, island beef, wild mountain vegetables, shochu aged in cedar casks. This is one of the best small hotels in Japan for this kind of group.

**The forest days split the group.** Half leave before dawn for the Jomon Sugi trail — 22 kilometers of ancient forestry railroad track through primordial cedar, arriving at a 3,000-year-old tree eight hours later. The trail is long but not technical; what makes it is the cumulative effect of walking through increasingly ancient forest as rain comes and goes. The other half walks Shiratani Unsuikyo Ravine — the forest Hayao Miyazaki studied to create Princess Mononoke, where every surface is smothered in moss, the light is green and diffuse, and it feels less like a hike than stepping inside the animation. Neither group is settling for second best.

**The wildlife day.** Drive the island circuit road and you encounter things unavailable elsewhere in Japan. Yaku macaques — an island subspecies of Japanese macaque — move through the forest in troops of 20–30, frequently crossing the road with the frank curiosity of animals that have decided you're neither threat nor reward. Yaku deer, affected by island dwarfism, are improbably small and completely unafraid. Yakusugi Land offers ancient cedar groves on easy looping trails, and the Oko no Taki waterfall rounds out the afternoon.

**The night on the beach.** Nagata Inakahama on Yakushima's northwest coast is one of the most important loggerhead sea turtle nesting beaches in the North Pacific. On a good late-June night, a female emerges from the sea — 150 kilograms, over a meter long — hauls herself up the sand in the darkness, digs a nest, and lays. You watch from close range with a guide, in near-total silence. The turtle pays no attention to you. The process takes over an hour. There is nothing quite like it.

### Kerama Islands (3 nights)

![White sand and clear turquoise water, Kerama Islands, Okinawa](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_500,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1514405658/okinawa/Okinawa1969_3)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/okinawa/okinawa/)*

The Kerama Islands are a national park 30–50km west of Naha — a cluster of small fishing islands surrounded by the clearest water in Japan. Visibility regularly runs 20–30 meters. Sea turtles are residents who graze coral at snorkeling depth. Furuzamami Beach on Zamami Island, where you base, has the kind of turquoise water you see in photographs and stop believing is real until you're standing in it.

![Crystal-clear tropical water off the Okinawa islands](https://res.cloudinary.com/jnto/image/upload/w_1400,h_500,c_fill,f_auto,fl_lossy,q_auto/v1514407221/okinawa/Okinawa1975_4)
*Photo: [JNTO](https://www.japan.travel/en/destinations/okinawa/okinawa/)*

Scuba divers find some of the best diving in Japan here — coral gardens, endemic sea turtles, excellent visibility. Snorkelers encounter the same turtles in shallow water. Glass-bottom boat tours, sea kayaking to neighboring beaches, mornings with fresh sashimi from the day's catch. The Keramas have no resort infrastructure — accommodation is small guesthouses and minshuku — and that is exactly right. The location is the luxury.

## Getting there and back

**SFO → Tokyo:** Nonstop on ANA, JAL, or United (~11 hrs) into Haneda — closer to Tokyo.

**Tokyo → Kagoshima:** ANA or JAL nonstop from Haneda (~1h40m). Multiple daily flights.

**Kagoshima → Yakushima:** High-speed ferry (Toppy or Rocket hydrofoil) from Kagoshima Port to Miyanoura, Yakushima (~1h50m). 3-4 departures per day. Practical for a group with luggage. The slower car ferry (~4 hours) is cheaper and weather-cancellation resistant — useful to know as backup. Allow buffer: both the ferry and the small propeller plane alternative can be cancelled in bad weather, and June weather on this coast is unpredictable.

**Total travel time Tokyo → Yakushima:** About 5-6 hours door to door on a good day. It is a travel day.

**Yakushima → Kerama:** The longest transit. Ferry back to Kagoshima (~1h50m), then fly Kagoshima → Naha (~1h15m), then high-speed ferry from Naha's Tomari Port to either Zamami or Tokashiki (~35-50 min). Full travel day — build this as a light arrival day.

**Naha → SFO:** Fly Naha to Haneda (~2.5 hrs), connect to nonstop SFO. Or fly Naha directly to Tokyo Narita on a budget carrier (Peach, Jetstar) and connect there. Book this well ahead for 6 seats.

## Day-by-day

### Day 1: Arrive Tokyo
Land, check in, easy nearby dinner, adjust.

### Day 2: Tokyo — focused
Morning: Coffee in Kiyosumi-Shirakawa (Juan-led walk, everyone welcome).
Afternoon: Shimokitazawa vintage/thrift for Steph and anyone interested; Ginza or Omotesando for Eliza; free choice for everyone else.
Evening: One strong group dinner — book ahead.

### Day 3: Tokyo — free
Full free day. Zoo (Eliza), running/gym (Phil, Christian), independent wandering, Kappabashi kitchen street for Juan, whatever the group needs.

### Day 4: Travel to Yakushima
Morning: Final Tokyo morning.
Afternoon: Fly Haneda → Kagoshima (~1h40m). Transfer to ferry port (bus or taxi, ~20 min). Take afternoon high-speed ferry to Yakushima (~1h50m).
Arrive Miyanoura by evening. Sankara sends a transfer. Easy first dinner at the hotel.

### Day 5: The Big Split
*4:30am:* Phil, Christian, Juan, Steph board the shuttle bus to the Jomon Sugi trailhead.
*8:00am:* Eliza and Lillian drive to Shiratani Unsuikyo Ravine. Morning in the Princess Mononoke forest. Long lunch. Afternoon: coastal drive, beach walk.
*Late afternoon:* Hikers return. Everyone exhausted and elated. Dinner at Sankara.

### Day 6: Wildlife and the island circuit
Relaxed morning. Drive the coast road — macaques and deer likely within the first hour. Yakusugi Land for the ancient cedar grove walks (easy, 1-2 hours, suitable for everyone). Afternoon: Oko no Taki waterfall. West coast sunset. Private onsen (book the kashikiri at Sankara for Juan & Steph; others can use the regular bath).

### Day 7: Snorkeling, coast, turtle night
Morning: Sea kayaking or snorkeling outing with a local operator (flying fish, reef fish, clear water). Option to rent glass-bottom kayaks independently.
Afternoon: Rest, swim, pool, Sankara.
Evening: Drive to Nagata Inakahama. Sea turtle tour (pre-booked). Return late.

### Day 8: Travel day — Yakushima to Kerama
Early morning: Return to Miyanoura port. High-speed ferry to Kagoshima.
Midday: Fly Kagoshima → Naha.
Afternoon: Take the Zamami Line ferry from Naha Tomari Port to Zamami (~50 min high-speed).
Evening: Arrive Zamami, check in, simple dinner.

### Day 9: Kerama — diving and the beach
Full day on Zamami. Phil: 2-tank guided dive (book ahead through the island's dive shops). Everyone else: snorkeling at Furuzamami Beach. Sea turtles are residents — encounter on the surface is very likely. Long lunch. Afternoon free.

### Day 10: Kerama — exploring
Morning: Glass-bottom boat rental, or kayak to a neighboring beach, or hire a local fishing boat for a morning tour.
Afternoon: Aharen Beach on Tokashiki (take the morning ferry, ~20 min) if the group wants a second beach. Or stay on Zamami and be slow.
Evening: Best dinner of the Kerama stay.

### Day 11: Naha
Morning ferry back to Naha. Check in at Naha hotel.
Afternoon: Kokusai-dori (Okinawa's main street food and shopping strip), Makishi Public Market (three floors, fresh seafood on the ground floor, cooked versions upstairs — bring your catch to the second floor and they'll cook it). Shuri Castle grounds (the Ryukyu palace, partially restored after the 2019 fire).
Juan: Find the best coffee in Naha. They are there.
Eliza: Naha's DFS Galleria for last-minute shopping.
Evening: Final dinner — Okinawan food, awamori (local rice spirit).

### Day 12: Fly home
Fly Naha → Haneda (~2.5 hrs), connect to SFO nonstop. Or fly via Narita.

## What to book before you leave

In approximate order of urgency:
1. **Sankara Hotel** — 3 villas, 4 nights. Books out months ahead.
2. **International Driving Permits** for anyone who'll drive on Yakushima. Get from AAA before departure.
3. **Yakushima car rentals** — 2 compact cars for the island portion. Small fleet, book early.
4. **Sea turtle tour** (Yakushima Sea Turtle Museum or local guide, Nagata Inakahama)
5. **Kerama guesthouse** — 3 rooms (or a house), 3 nights on Zamami
6. **Kerama dive operator** — guided 2-tank dive for Phil (and anyone else who dives)
7. **Domestic flights:** Tokyo HND → Kagoshima, Kagoshima → Naha (book together as a 6-person itinerary)
8. **Ferries:** Kagoshima↔Yakushima (Cosmo Line, Toppy), Naha→Zamami (Zamami Line)
9. **Naha hotel** — 1 night on Day 11
10. **Jomon Sugi shuttle bus** — check if a permit/reservation system is in place by 2026 (there have been ongoing discussions about introducing one)

## Key risks

**Weather on Yakushima is the biggest variable.** The island is one of the wettest places in Japan — average June rainfall at sea level is 450-650mm. This is not a reason not to go. The forest is most atmospheric in rain. The Jomon Sugi hike is routinely done in rain by prepared hikers. Pack proper rain gear (full jacket + pants + waterproof boots) and embrace it. However: ferry cancellations are a real possibility in bad weather. Do not schedule Yakushima as a tight layover — the 4-night stay gives you one buffer night.

**Lillian / pregnancy:** No diving (pressure changes), no onsen (hyperthermia risk), avoid extremely strenuous hikes. Everything else on this itinerary — Shiratani forest walk, sea turtle tour, Kerama snorkeling, boat trips — is appropriate. Confirm all activities with her OB before the trip.

**Tattoo policy:** Solved by Sankara's private villas. On Kerama, the small guesthouses typically do not have communal onsen (the islands aren't known for hot springs), so this is essentially a non-issue for the Kerama portion.

**June timing precision:** For Kerama to be genuinely beautiful, you want late June (after ~June 20) when Okinawa's tsuyu has ended. Sea turtle nesting is active from late May so June timing is fine for that. If you must travel in early June, Yakushima is unaffected but Kerama may have more rain and slightly reduced snorkeling visibility.

**Naha departure day is short** — you're flying home from Naha, and Day 11 is mostly a half-day in the city before sleeping. This is not a major issue (everyone has been to Japan before; Naha for half a day is a pleasant layover not a destination), but manage expectations.
