# Option B — Bergen to Alesund

**Bergen (2N) → Sognefjord / Flam (2N) → Geirangerfjord (2N) → Alesund (2N)**
Shared days: ~8 nights, June 9–17

---

## The idea

A one-way road trip up Norway's fjord coast. Fly into Bergen, drive north through Sognefjord and Geirangerfjord, and end in Alesund. The group splits from Alesund on June 17–18:

- **P&L** fly Alesund → Bodo → Lofoten (connection via Oslo or Bodo direct — slightly fewer options than from Bergen)
- **C&E** fly Alesund → their next city (via Oslo)
- **J&S** fly onward or take the Hurtigruten coastal ferry for a dramatic departure

### Why this shape works

- You see the full range of Norway's fjord landscape — from Sognefjord in the south to Geirangerfjord in the north
- Geirangerfjord is arguably Norway's most dramatic scenery (UNESCO World Heritage, the Seven Sisters waterfall, narrow emerald-green water)
- Alesund is a gorgeous Art Nouveau town rebuilt after a 1904 fire — great for the final nights
- One-way means no backtracking — every day you wake up somewhere new

---

## Day-by-day sketch

### Days 1–2: Bergen (June 9–11)

- Arrive, recover from jetlag
- Bryggen wharf, fish market, Floibanen funicular
- Mt. Floyen or Ulriken hike
- Coffee, shopping, and dinner in the city

### Days 3–4: Sognefjord / Flam (June 11–13)

- Drive or train to Flam via the scenic route
- Flam Railway, Aurlandsfjord kayaking, Stegastein viewpoint
- Nærøyfjord boat trip (UNESCO)
- Hike options: Rimstigen or gentler fjord walks

### Days 5–6: Geirangerfjord (June 13–15)

- Drive north via Stryn or Hellesylt (scenic mountain passes, possible snow walls in June)
- **Geirangerfjord cruise** — the Seven Sisters and Bridal Veil waterfalls from the water
- **Dalsnibba viewpoint** (1,500m) — panoramic view of the entire fjord system
- **Hike to Skageflå** — an abandoned mountain farm clinging to a cliff above the fjord
- Possible kayaking on Geirangerfjord (less crowded than Aurlandsfjord, more dramatic)

### Days 7–8: Alesund (June 15–17)

- Drive to Alesund (~2.5h from Geiranger via the Trollstigen road if open — one of Norway's most famous mountain passes with hairpin bends and waterfalls)
- **Trollstigen** — if the road is open in mid-June, this is an unforgettable drive
- Alesund town: Art Nouveau architecture, climb Aksla for the sunset panorama
- **Atlantic Sea Park** (Atlanterhavsparken) — one of Northern Europe's largest aquariums, open-air seal and penguin exhibits (good for the animal lovers)
- Island-hopping by boat to Runde (puffin colony, ~500,000 seabirds in nesting season)
- Seafood dinners along the harbor

### June 17–18: Group splits

- P&L: fly Alesund → Bodo (via Oslo, ~4–5h total travel)
- C&E: fly Alesund → onward
- J&S: continue trip

---

## Strengths

- You see both Sognefjord AND Geirangerfjord — the two most iconic fjord landscapes
- Trollstigen pass is a once-in-a-lifetime drive (if open)
- Alesund is a beautiful, underrated endpoint — Art Nouveau architecture, great aquarium, puffin islands nearby
- One-way road trip means maximum variety, no repeated scenery
- Runde puffin island is a genuine wildlife highlight

## Tradeoffs

- Alesund has fewer direct flights out — P&L's Lofoten connection requires going through Oslo, adding a few hours
- More driving overall than Option A
- Flights into Bergen + out of Alesund may cost more than round-trip Bergen (one-way rental also has drop-off fees)
- You skip Hardangerfjord and Trolltunga
- Trollstigen may still be closed in early-mid June (usually opens late May, but snow years can delay)

## Rough cost signals

- Flights SFO → Bergen: $800–1,200; Alesund → various: $150–400
- One-way car rental with drop-off fee: ~$100–150/day per car
- Accommodation: similar to Option A, Geiranger area can be pricier in peak season
- P&L flights Alesund → Bodo (via Oslo): ~$150–300
