# Option C — Alesund to Bergen

**Alesund (2N) → Geirangerfjord (2N) → Sognefjord / Flam (2N) → Bergen (2N)**
Shared days: ~8 nights, June 9–17

---

## The idea

The reverse of Option B. Fly into Alesund, drive south through Geirangerfjord and Sognefjord, and end in Bergen. The group splits from Bergen on June 17–18:

- **P&L** fly Bergen → Bodo → Lofoten (easy — multiple daily flights)
- **C&E** fly Bergen → their next city (excellent connections)
- **J&S** continue their European trip from Bergen (direct flights everywhere)

### Why this shape works

- You end in Bergen, which is the best hub for splitting: P&L get the easiest Lofoten connection, and everyone else gets the most flight options
- You start with the dramatic scenery (Geirangerfjord, Trollstigen) while energy is high
- Bergen is a great city to end in — good restaurants, shopping, and a natural "wind-down" after the fjords
- Arriving in Alesund first means the "smaller airport, longer connection" happens when everyone is together and fresh, not when people are splitting and tired

### The Alesund connection tradeoff

Flying into Alesund from SFO requires a double connection (e.g., SFO → London → Oslo → Alesund, or SFO → Amsterdam → Oslo → Alesund). This adds 2–4 hours of travel compared to flying into Bergen. But:

- Everyone is together, so the connection is a shared hassle, not individual logistics
- You only deal with this once, at the start
- At the end, Bergen's connections are better for everyone's diverging plans

## Day-by-day sketch

### Days 1–2: Alesund (June 9–11)

- Arrive, recover (you'll be more tired from the longer connection — good reason to have a mellow start)
- Alesund town: Art Nouveau architecture walking tour, climb Aksla for the panoramic view
- **Atlantic Sea Park** — aquarium with outdoor seal and penguin exhibits
- **Runde Island** day trip — boat to one of Norway's largest seabird colonies (puffins, gannets, sea eagles nesting in June)
- Seafood dinners on the harbor

### Days 3–4: Geirangerfjord (June 11–13)

- Drive south toward Geiranger via **Trollstigen** (if open — hairpin mountain road with waterfalls)
- **Geirangerfjord cruise** — Seven Sisters waterfall, Bridal Veil, sheer green cliffs
- **Dalsnibba viewpoint** — 1,500m panoramic overlook
- **Hike to Skageflå** — abandoned cliff farm above the fjord
- Kayaking on the fjord

### Days 5–6: Sognefjord / Flam (June 13–15)

- Drive south to the Sognefjord area
- **Flam Railway** — iconic scenic train ride
- **Nærøyfjord** cruise (UNESCO World Heritage)
- **Stegastein viewpoint** — dramatic fjord overlook
- Hike options: Rimstigen (challenging, big views) or easier walks
- Aurlandsfjord kayaking

### Days 7–8: Bergen (June 15–17)

- Drive to Bergen (~2.5h)
- Bryggen wharf, fish market, Floibanen funicular
- Mt. Floyen or Ulriken hike
- Shopping, coffee, exploring the city
- Final group dinner — Bergen has the best restaurant scene of any city on these itineraries

### June 17–18: Group splits

- P&L: fly Bergen → Bodo, then to Lofoten (easy and frequent)
- C&E: fly Bergen → onward (lots of direct European connections)
- J&S: continue from Bergen

---

## Strengths

- Best splitting logistics — Bergen is the easiest city for everyone to go their separate ways
- P&L get the best possible Lofoten connection
- You see both Geirangerfjord and Sognefjord
- Trollstigen drive (if open)
- Ends in Bergen, which is the best city for dining, shopping, and a satisfying last couple of nights
- Puffins on Runde early in the trip

## Tradeoffs

- The connection into Alesund is longer and more expensive for everyone (double connection)
- More total driving than Option A
- One-way car rental drop-off fees
- You skip Hardangerfjord and Trolltunga (same as Option B)
- Trollstigen may still be closed in early-mid June

---

## Rough cost signals

- Flights SFO → Alesund (double connection): $900–1,400
- One-way car rental with drop-off: ~$100–150/day per car
- Accommodation: similar to other options
- P&L flights Bergen → Bodo: ~$100–200 (cheapest of all three options for this leg)

---

## Comparison to Option B

Options B and C cover the same ground in opposite directions. The key difference is where you start and where you split:

| | Option B (Bergen → Alesund) | Option C (Alesund → Bergen) |
|---|---|---|
| Arrival connection | Easier (Bergen) | Harder (Alesund) |
| Splitting logistics | Harder (from Alesund) | Easier (from Bergen) |
| P&L Lofoten flights | Via Oslo, more $$ | Direct Bergen→Bodo, less $$ |
| C&E/J&S onward flights | Fewer options from Alesund | Many options from Bergen |
| Trip arc | Builds from city to dramatic scenery | Starts dramatic, winds down in a city |
